# 🪤 Chapter 3 — Test Leadership and Management: Exam Traps

## 🪤 Trap 1: Managing Instead of Leading

**Trap:** In agile, test leaders serve and enable rather than command and control.

**Example question:**
> A test manager assigns specific test cases to each tester daily and checks completion every evening. This is:
>
> A. Effective agile test management
> B. Micromanagement; in agile, teams self-organize and the leader removes blockers
> C. Only wrong for senior testers
> D. Only wrong for remote teams

**Correct answer:** B — Agile leaders serve and enable, not micromanage.

---

## 🪤 Trap 2: Solving Problems for the Team

**Trap:** Coaching draws solutions from the team; solving for them creates dependency.

**Example question:**
> A tester asks how to test a complex scenario. The leader gives a complete solution immediately. The consequence is:
>
> A. Faster resolution
> B. The tester learns less and becomes dependent on the leader for future decisions
> C. Only slower next time
> D. Only more documentation

**Correct answer:** B — Coaching develops independent thinkers.

---

## 🪤 Trap 3: Ignoring Team Development Stage

**Trap:** New teams need direction; performing teams need autonomy. Applying the wrong style causes friction.

**Example question:**
> A newly formed test team is given full autonomy with no guidance. They struggle. The issue is:
>
> A. The team is incompetent
> B. New teams (Forming stage) need more structure and direction before autonomy
> C. Only that they need better tools
> D. Only that they need more time

**Correct answer:** B — Match leadership style to team development stage.

---

## 🪤 Trap 4: Blaming Individuals for Quality Issues

**Trap:** Quality issues are system problems. Blame creates fear and hides root causes.

**Example question:**
> A production bug escapes because a tester "didn't test edge cases." The investigation reveals no time was allocated for testing. The real issue is:
>
> A. The tester's fault
> B. A system problem — testing time wasn't planned; blameless analysis is needed
> C. Only a training issue
> D. Only a documentation issue

**Correct answer:** B — Focus on system fixes, not personal blame.

---

## 🪤 Trap 5: Treating Impediments as Someone Else's Problem

**Trap:** Test leaders must actively remove blockers, not just report them.

**Example question:**
> A test lead reports slow environments to management monthly but never follows up. The team remains blocked. The lead should:
>
> A. Continue reporting — management will fix it eventually
> B. Escalate actively, propose solutions, and track resolution until the blocker is removed
> C. Only tell the team to wait
> D. Only find workarounds

**Correct answer:** B — Leaders actively remove impediments.
