# Chapter 6 — Organizational Culture and Quality: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Thinking Culture Can Be Changed by Policy

**Trap:** Culture changes through behavior modeling and experience, not memos or mandates.

**Example question:**
> A company emails all employees stating "quality is now everyone's responsibility." The likely result is:
>
> A. Immediate culture change
> B. Minimal change — culture shifts through behavior modeling, training, and shared experiences, not announcements
> C. Only slower development
> D. Only more meetings

**Correct answer:** B — Culture change requires sustained behavioral change, not memos.

---

## Trap 2: Blaming Culture Without Changing Systems

**Trap:** "It's a culture problem" is often an excuse. Change systems (processes, incentives) to change culture.

**Example question:**
> A team blames "quality culture" for frequent production issues. Investigation shows no time for testing in sprint planning. The real issue is:
>
> A. Bad people
> B. A system problem — sprint planning doesn't allocate testing time; changing the process changes behavior
> C. Only training needed
> D. Only a tool issue

**Correct answer:** B — Change systems to change culture; blaming people doesn't help.

---

## Trap 3: Separating Quality from Delivery Speed

**Trap:** Quality and speed are not trade-offs when quality is built in. Finding defects late slows everything.

**Example question:**
> A team skips testing to meet a deadline. The likely outcome is:
>
> A. Faster delivery
> B. Rework, hotfixes, and customer issues that ultimately delay the true delivery of working software
> C. Only fewer bugs
> D. Only happier developers

**Correct answer:** B — Skipping testing creates technical debt that slows future delivery.

---

## Trap 4: Making Retrospectives Optional

**Trap:** Retrospectives are the engine of continuous improvement. Skipping them stops improvement.

**Example question:**
> A team cancels retrospectives because "we don't have time." The consequence is:
>
> A. More time for development
> B. Loss of the primary mechanism for identifying and implementing process improvements
> C. Only fewer meetings
> D. Only faster sprints

**Correct answer:** B — Retrospectives drive continuous improvement.

---

## Trap 5: Treating Testers as Second-Class Contributors

**Trap:** When testers are marginalized, quality suffers. Whole-team quality requires equal respect.

**Example question:**
> Testers are not invited to sprint planning and only receive stories after development is complete. The impact is:
>
> A. Faster development
> B. Testing becomes bottleneck; quality perspective missing from design; team doesn't own quality
> C. Only more documentation
> D. Only fewer meetings

**Correct answer:** B — Testers must be involved early for whole-team quality.
