# CT-ATLaS Glossary

## A

- **Agile at Scale** — Applying agile principles across multiple teams and the enterprise
- **Agile Release Train (ART)** — SAFe concept: long-lived team of teams delivering value

## B

- **Burn-Down Chart** — Visual representation of work remaining in a sprint
- **Burn-Up Chart** — Visual representation of work completed toward a goal

## C

- **Communities of Practice (CoP)** — Groups sharing knowledge across teams
- **Continuous Integration (CI)** — Merging code frequently with automated builds and tests

## D

- **Definition of Done (DoD)** — Shared agreement on when work is complete
- **Definition of Ready (DoR)** — Shared agreement on when work can start

## E

- **Epic** — Large body of work that can be broken into stories
- **Enterprise Agile** — Applying agile across the entire organization

## F

- **Feature Team** — Cross-functional team delivering complete features
- **Framework** — Structured approach (SAFe, LeSS, Scrum@Scale)

## I

- **Inspect and Adapt** — Regular review and improvement of processes
- **Integration Point** — Where multiple teams' work comes together

## K

- **Kanban** — Visual workflow management with WIP limits

## L

- **Large-Scale Scrum (LeSS)** — Framework for scaling Scrum to multiple teams
- **Lead Time** — Time from request to delivery

## M

- **Metrics** — Quantitative measures used to track progress and quality
- **Minimum Viable Product (MVP)** — Smallest product version delivering value

## P

- **Program Increment (PI)** — SAFe term: timeboxed planning interval (typically 8-12 weeks)
- **Psychological Safety** — Team climate where people feel safe to take risks

## R

- **Release Train Engineer (RTE)** — SAFe role: servant leader for the ART
- **Retrospective** — Regular team meeting to improve processes

## S

- **SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)** — Framework for enterprise-scale agile
- **Scrum of Scrums** — Coordination meeting across multiple Scrum teams
- **Servant Leadership** — Leadership focused on supporting team success
- **Spotify Model** — Squads, tribes, chapters, guilds organizational model
- **Sprint** — Timeboxed iteration (typically 1-4 weeks)

## T

- **Test Coach** — Role guiding teams toward quality practices
- **Tribe** — Spotify model: collection of squads working in related areas

## V

- **Velocity** — Amount of work completed per sprint (used for planning, not comparison)
- **Value Stream** — Sequence of activities delivering value to customer

## W

- **Whole Team Approach** — Everyone shares responsibility for quality
- **WIP Limit** — Work in progress limit to improve flow
