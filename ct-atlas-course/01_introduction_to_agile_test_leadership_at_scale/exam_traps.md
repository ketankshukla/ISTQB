# Chapter 1 — Introduction to Agile Test Leadership at Scale: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Thinking Agile at Scale is Just Big Agile

**Trap:** Scaling requires new structures (ARTs, chapters, tribes) and coordination mechanisms, not just more teams doing Scrum.

**Example question:**
> A company has 20 teams doing Scrum independently with no coordination. They have:
>
> A. Scaled agile
> B. Many agile teams, not scaled agile — integration, alignment, and quality coordination are missing
> C. Only a communication problem
> D. Only a tooling problem

**Correct answer:** B — Scaling requires coordination structures, not just more teams.

---

## Trap 2: Confusing SAFe, LeSS, and Spotify

**Trap:** SAFe = structured, hierarchical, PI planning. LeSS = lightweight, one PO, shared sprints. Spotify = autonomous squads, tribes, chapters.

**Example question:**
> A framework with autonomous squads, tribes, and chapters is:
>
> A. SAFe
> B. LeSS
> C. The Spotify model
> D. Scrum of Scrums

**Correct answer:** C — Squads, tribes, chapters = Spotify model.

---

## Trap 3: Eliminating Test Managers in Scaling

**Trap:** Agile at scale still needs test leadership for coordination, standards, and cross-team quality.

**Example question:**
> In scaled agile, the role of test manager should:
>
> A. Be eliminated — teams are self-organizing
> B. Evolve into coordination, coaching, and quality standards, not daily task assignment
> C. Only manage manual testers
> D. Only write test scripts

**Correct answer:** B — Test leadership evolves but remains essential at scale.

---

## Trap 4: Ignoring Integration Testing in Scaling

**Trap:** When multiple teams integrate work, system-level testing is critical and often missed.

**Example question:**
> Each team in an ART has 100% unit test coverage and passes all sprint tests. At the system demo:
>
> A. System integration is guaranteed to work
> B. Integration issues may still exist; system-level validation is still needed
> C. Only UI testing remains
> D. Only performance testing remains

**Correct answer:** B — Team-level quality ≠ system-level quality.

---

## Trap 5: Using Velocity for Cross-Team Comparison

**Trap:** Velocity is team-specific and depends on story point calibration. Comparing velocities is meaningless and harmful.

**Example question:**
> Management ranks teams by velocity to identify "high performers." This is:
>
> A. Effective management
> B. Harmful — velocity is for planning, not comparison; teams have different baselines
> C) Only wrong for new teams
> D) Only wrong for remote teams

**Correct answer:** B — Velocity is not comparable across teams.
