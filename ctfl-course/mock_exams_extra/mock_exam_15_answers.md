# 📝 Mock Exam 15 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.2, K1)*
**Testing** is a **subset of quality control** (product-oriented) activities. It does not replace QA, and QA is process-oriented (it does not execute software to find defects).

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
Testing before release aims to **reduce the risk of operational failures** — not to leave defects undiscovered or skip stakeholder input.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
Defects concentrating in a few areas is the principle "**defects cluster together**."

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
**Not every defect causes a failure** — the defective code may never execute, or its triggering conditions may never occur.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
Turning test conditions into test cases is part of **test design**.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.5, K2)*
The **test management role** defines the **strategy/approach and monitors progress**, rather than writing code or designing every test personally.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
In regulated industries, **traceability demonstrates that requirements** (including safety/compliance ones) are **covered by tests**, supporting audits.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.1, K2)*
Testing provides **objective information about quality and risk** to support decisions — it does not guarantee success or replace development.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
**BDD** uses **business-readable examples** (Given/When/Then) and is a test-first approach.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
A good practice is to begin **test analysis and design during the corresponding development activity**, finding defects early.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.1, K2)*
A drawback of **sequential** models is that **early-phase defects may be found late**, when they are more expensive to fix.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.3, K2)*
After a fix, **confirmation testing** verifies the fix and **regression testing** checks for unintended side effects — they work together.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
Checking the app on iOS and Android tests **portability/compatibility**, a **non-functional** characteristic.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**UAT** is performed by **representatives of the users/business** to validate fitness for use.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
Static testing of requirements lets you **find defects before any code exists**, an early-feedback advantage.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.5, K1)*
**Lack of training and unclear roles** undermine reviews; preparation, clear objectives, and management support help them succeed.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.2.2, K2)*
**Individual review (preparation)** is where reviewers identify potential defects on their own, before any meeting.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.3, K2)*
**Static testing** can find defects that are **hard to detect dynamically** — unreachable code, inconsistent or ambiguous requirements — without execution.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
Effective testing **combines** black-box (requirements coverage) with white-box (code-coverage gaps), and includes experience-based techniques.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
**Exploratory testing** excels at **rapidly learning** a new/poorly documented system and surfacing important issues; it complements planning rather than replacing it.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** is an **experience-based** technique.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.3, K2)*
A good checklist is grounded in **experience, standards, and known risks**, and is **kept up to date** to remain effective.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.2, K2)*
The **Given/When/Then** logout example is **scenario-oriented**. The others are rule-oriented or non-functional statements.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
The **conversation** matters because shared understanding and many details **emerge through discussion**, not solely from the brief card.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
For an exact length of 8, the boundaries are around 8: test **7 (too short), 8 (valid), 9 (too long)**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
**1-switch coverage** requires covering **each valid sequence of two consecutive transitions**, whereas 0-switch covers single transitions.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.3.1, K3)*

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.6, K1)*
The **test pyramid** guides the **proportion of automated tests at different levels** (many low-level, fewer high-level).

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.7, K2)*
**Usability, exploratory, and certain non-functional tests** "critique the product" in the testing quadrants.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
A test plan manages testing by defining **what, how, when, and by whom** it is done.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.3, K2)*
Risk analysis influences **which test types/levels to apply and how to prioritize** — focusing effort by risk.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
Insufficient **test environments** is a **project risk** affecting the project's ability to deliver.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.3, K2)*
Reporting a blocking **impediment** so it can be resolved is good status communication — not hiding the problem.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
Good practice is to recognize **duplicate** reports and **link/merge** them, avoiding wasted effort and confusion.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
