### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
Testing **prevents defects** by involving testers **early** (e.g., reviewing requirements), which avoids introducing defects in the first place.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.5.1, K1)*
**Attention to detail** is a key generic tester skill — spotting small discrepancies that lead to defects.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
The **pesticide paradox**: repeating identical tests eventually stops finding new defects, so tests must be reviewed and updated.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
An **error** is the **human mistake** — here, the developer misunderstanding the formula. The config typo/wrong total are defects/failures.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
Identifying testable features and **defining test conditions** is part of **test analysis**.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.5, K2)*
**Designing and executing specific test cases** is a **testing role** task; policy, budget, and strategy belong to test management.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
Good **traceability** makes it easy to assess the **impact of a requirement change** on the associated tests.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.1, K2)*
Testing is necessary to **find defects so they can be fixed before release, reducing risk** — it cannot prove the absence of defects.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
In **ATDD**, acceptance tests are **agreed collaboratively** and **drive development**.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
A good practice in every SDLC is that **each test activity has a defined objective**.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.1, K2)*
In Agile, defects are **found and fixed within the same short cycle**, giving fast feedback.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
Measuring search time under load tests a **performance** quality characteristic — **non-functional testing**.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Acceptance testing** validates whether the **business needs** are met and the system is ready for use.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.2.3, K2)*
Automating regression lets it be **run frequently and cheaply after changes** — it still needs maintenance and does not replace confirmation testing.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.2.3, K1)*
The **scribe** documents the **issues and decisions** raised during the review.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
Static testing can be applied to any readable work product, such as a **test plan document** — no execution required.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.2, K2)*
The **earlier** a defect is found, the **cheaper** it generally is to fix — a core benefit of static testing.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.2.2, K2)*
**Individual review (preparation)** is where reviewers examine the work product **on their own**.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
