# 📝 Mock Exam 19 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
Checking whether **specified requirements** are met is **verification** ("are we building the product right?").

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.1, K1)*
Explaining a defect **calmly and factually** shows strong **interpersonal/communication skills**, which reduce defensiveness.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
"**Testing shows the presence, not the absence, of defects**" — it reduces uncertainty but cannot prove none remain.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
Failures can also arise from **environmental conditions** (radiation, hardware faults, etc.), not only from code defects.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
Traceability to results lets you **report coverage and the status** of each requirement/test condition.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
**Test planning** is the first activity in the test process.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Test logs** are produced during **test execution**.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.2, K2)*
Adapting the process to the situation reflects that **testing is context dependent**.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
TDD, ATDD, and BDD all **use tests/examples to drive and clarify development**.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
Testing should **fit the chosen SDLC** and be planned accordingly.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.4, K2)*
In **CI/CD**, automated tests run **automatically on commit/integration**, giving fast feedback.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
The typical order (low → high) is **component → integration → system → acceptance**.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
Behavior under expected load is **performance (non-functional)** testing.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.3, K2)*
Rerunning the failed test to confirm the fix is **confirmation (re-)testing**.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
A **user manual/documentation** is a static work product suitable for review; the others require execution to observe.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.5, K1)*
**Reviewing in small, manageable chunks** contributes to review success; overload, no preparation, and unclear objectives hurt it.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.3, K2)*
**Static and dynamic testing are complementary**, finding **different kinds of defects** — both are valuable.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.2.2, K2)*
**Planning** decides the review's **objectives, scope, and participants**.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
**Experience-based** techniques shine when **specs are sparse or time is short**, drawing on tester expertise.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
**Branch (decision) coverage subsumes statement coverage** — achieving 100% branch coverage guarantees 100% statement coverage, but not vice versa.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
Exploratory testing **adapts in real time** to what the tester learns about the system.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
Error guessing targets **empty inputs, boundaries, and invalid data** — common defect triggers.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.2, K2)*
Good acceptance criteria are **clear, testable, and agreed** by stakeholders.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
The **conversation** fleshes out the **details and shared understanding** of the story.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: D  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
Valid settings are 10–30°C, so **35** is **invalid**.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Two-value BVA** at the upper boundary 30 tests the boundary and its nearest neighbor outside: **30 and 31**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
With 2 conditions there are 4 combinations; if **one is impossible**, at most **3** feasible rules remain.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
"Open" when already Open has **no defined transition**, so it is an **invalid transition** — useful for negative state-transition testing.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.3.1, K3)*
One test that makes the IF **true** executes the 5 sequential statements plus the one inside the IF — **1** test case for full statement coverage. (Branch coverage would need 2.)

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.6, K1)*
The **base** of the pyramid is **fast, numerous unit/component tests**.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
A test plan documents the **entry and exit criteria** (among scope, approach, schedule, and risks).

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.3, K2)*
Good status communication is **timely** and **highlights risks and issues** needing attention.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.4, K2)*
Risk-based testing **focuses limited resources** where they reduce the most risk.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
**Ambiguous requirements** threatening the delivery of the right product is a **project risk**.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.4.1, K2)*
Without configuration management, you risk **testing the wrong version** or being **unable to reproduce** a defect.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
The **status** field tracks the defect's **lifecycle** (new, assigned, fixed, closed, etc.).

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
A **defect/test management tool** logs, tracks, and reports **defects and test progress**.

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
