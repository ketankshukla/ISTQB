# 📝 Mock Exam 8 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
A typical objective is to **verify and validate** that the software meets specified requirements. Increasing defects, avoiding decisions, or removing QA are not objectives.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.1, K1)*
**Communication skills** help testers write clear defect reports and interact with stakeholders. Programming alone, graphic design, and accounting are not the key skill here.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
"**Testing is context dependent**": the approach, depth, and techniques vary with context — a safety-critical system is tested far more rigorously than a simple website.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
A **failure** results from executing a **defect** in the software (and occasionally from environmental conditions). It is not caused solely by the tester or documentation.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.5, K2)*
The **test management role** plans, monitors, controls, and reports on testing. Designing/executing individual tests is the testing role; writing production code is development.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
**Test analysis** answers "**what to test**" by identifying test conditions from the test basis.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
Good **traceability** lets the team quickly find the **test cases affected** by a changed requirement, so they can be updated — supporting impact analysis.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.1, K2)*
Testing supports success by **providing information for informed decisions** and **reducing the risk** of costly operational failures — not by guaranteeing perfection.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
**BDD** uses business-readable examples (Given/When/Then) to drive development and testing and to build shared understanding.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
A good practice is to **begin test analysis and design early**, during the corresponding development activity, to find defects sooner.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.5, K2)*
**Shift-left** moves test activities **earlier** to find defects sooner — it does not move testing later, cut testers, or skip static testing.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Component integration testing** focuses on the **interactions and interfaces** between integrated components.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
Checking a correct invoice total tests **what the system does** against requirements — **functional testing**.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.3.1, K2)*
**Upgrading a dependency** of an operational system is a **modification** that triggers **maintenance testing**. The other options are initial development or project events.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.3, K1)*
The **review leader** takes overall responsibility for the review and decides whether it can proceed to the next step.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
A **requirements document written before any code** can only be examined statically — there is nothing to execute yet. The other options require a running system.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.2, K2)*
**Static testing** finds defects **early** and improves the **consistency, completeness, and clarity** of work products; it does more than confirm compilation.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.2, K2)*
In **individual review (preparation)**, reviewers examine the work product **on their own** and note potential defects and questions to raise.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
**Black-box** techniques work from the specification and **do not require source-code access**. White-box uses structure; experience-based uses tester knowledge.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
White-box testing reveals **coverage gaps** — code never executed by the black-box tests — adding assurance that the structure is exercised.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.3, K2)*
Using a **maintenance checklist** during reviews is **checklist-based (experience-based) testing**.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
**Exploratory testing** designs and executes tests **dynamically** as the tester learns, unlike scripted testing where cases are pre-written. It can be time-boxed and is informative.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.5.2, K2)*
A single explicit rule ("lock after 3 failed attempts") is a **rule-oriented** acceptance criterion.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
The **Three Amigos** conversation builds a **shared understanding** of the story across business, development, and testing viewpoints.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: C  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
The valid length range is 1–20, so a **0-character (empty) string** lies in an **invalid** partition. Lengths 5, 10, and 20 are valid.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Two-value BVA** at the upper boundary 20 tests the boundary and its nearest neighbor outside the partition: **20 and 21**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: C  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
The **full** table with 3 binary conditions has 2³ = **8** rules; the infeasible combination would be removed only during simplification.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
There are **3 valid transitions** (login, logout, timeout); 0-switch coverage requires exercising all **3** at least once.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.2, K3)*
Two test cases cover all four branch outcomes: **x = 20** makes D1 true (y=1) and then D2 true; **x = 5** makes D1 false (y=2) and then D2 false. So **2** test cases.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.6, K1)*
The **test pyramid** concentrates automated tests at the **bottom (unit/component)** level, where they are fast, cheap, and stable.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.7, K2)*
The **testing quadrants** combine **business-facing/technology-facing** with **supporting-the-team/critiquing-the-product** to ensure a balanced test mix.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
A **test plan** coordinates testing by **communicating the approach, scope, schedule, and responsibilities** to stakeholders.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.3, K2)*
For a **high-risk** feature, apply **more rigorous techniques and greater coverage** — concentrate effort where failure impact is highest.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
**Unclear requirements** threatening delivery of the right product is a **project risk** (a risk to the project's success), distinct from a defect in the product.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.3, K2)*
Good status communication is **tailored to the audience** and highlights **risks and needed decisions** — not jargon for all, omitted risks, or unrecorded verbal updates.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*
Risk-based prioritization runs the **highest-risk tests first**, so the most important risks are covered even if time runs out.

### ✔️ Q37 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.1, K3)*
Risk level = likelihood × impact = 2 × 3 = **6**.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
**Severity** (impact) and **priority** (urgency) together support **triage** of a defect's importance and the order in which it should be fixed.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
A **CI tool** that builds and runs tests on each commit is part of **test execution/automation tooling** in the CI/CD pipeline.

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
Correct pairing: a **benefit** is fast feedback; a **risk** is the **maintenance cost** as the system changes. The other options misstate automation's costs and limits.
