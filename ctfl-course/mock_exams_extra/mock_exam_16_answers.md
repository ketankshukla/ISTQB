# 📝 Mock Exam 16 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
A typical objective is to **check that the test object is complete and behaves as stakeholders expect** — not to hide defects or skip evaluation.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.2, K1)*
In the **whole-team approach**, testers, developers, and the business **share quality goals** and collaborate.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
Writing new tests counters the **pesticide paradox**, in which repeated identical tests stop finding new defects.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
A **defect** is a flaw in a work product — here, an **incorrect SQL query in the codebase**. The reasoning mistake is an error; the runtime wrong result is a failure.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
**Test completion** produces the completion report and captures lessons learned.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Test planning** produces the **test plan and schedule**. Logs, defect reports, and actual results come from execution.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.2, K2)*
The **level of formality** depends on context: domain, risk, and regulatory requirements.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.3, K2)*
Too much independence can cause **isolation, communication problems, and reduced developer ownership** of quality.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
Involving testers **early in reviews of the test basis** is a good practice in all SDLCs.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
**ATDD** has the business/customer collaborate to define **acceptance tests before coding**.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.5, K2)*
**Shift-left** leads to **earlier defect detection and faster feedback**, lowering the cost of fixes.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**System testing** focuses on the **behavior and capabilities of the complete system**, often via end-to-end scenarios.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
**Stress testing** examines behavior beyond normal load — a **non-functional (performance-related)** test type.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.3.1, K2)*
A **new regulation requiring changes to a live system** triggers **maintenance testing** (a modification). The others are initial development or project events.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.2.3, K1)*
The **scribe (recorder)** documents the decisions and defects raised during the review.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.1, K1)*
**Frequent demos, reviews, and short iterations** enable continuous stakeholder feedback in Agile.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.2, K2)*
**Static testing** reduces cost because **early defect detection** avoids costlier downstream fixes and rework.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.4, K2)*
From least to most formal: **informal review → walkthrough → technical review → inspection**.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
**Black-box** techniques derive tests from **requirements, specifications, or behavioral models**, not from code or gut feeling.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
**Branch coverage** ensures both the **true and false outcomes** of decisions are tested.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** is the experience-based technique relying on intuition about likely faults.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
**Exploratory testing** is **simultaneous learning, design, and execution**, guided by charters — not random clicking or scripted testing.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.3, K3)*
**ATDD** reduces ambiguity by turning acceptance criteria into **concrete, agreed examples/tests before development**.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
"**Valuable**" (INVEST) means the story **delivers value to a user or customer**.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
**0 (below), 50 (valid), 101 (above)** gives one value from each partition. Option B uses only valid/boundary values; C is all valid; D is all below.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Three-value BVA** at the lower boundary 1 tests the boundary and both neighbors: **0, 1, 2**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
A decision table ensures **all combinations of the specified conditions** are considered, including those easily overlooked.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
There are **3 valid transitions** (New→Paid, Paid→Shipped, Shipped→Delivered); 0-switch coverage requires all **3**.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.2, K3)*
The single decision needs TRUE and FALSE outcomes → **2** test cases (e.g., a = 1 and a = -1) for branch coverage.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.3.1, K1)*
The **number of defects by severity** is a defect-related test metric; the others are unrelated to testing.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
A test plan should be **communicated to relevant stakeholders** so expectations and responsibilities are clear.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.3, K2)*
Meeting **coverage targets with zero open critical defects** is an **exit criterion**. A deployed build, baselined requirements, and ready test data are entry criteria.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.4, K2)*
With **uniformly low risk**, testing can be **lighter**, matching the risk — though never thoughtlessly skipped.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
A volatile **external dependency** threatening delivery is a **project risk**.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.2, K2)*
An **executive summary** gives a **high-level overview of status, key risks, and recommendations** — not full detail or raw logs.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
