# 📝 Mock Exam 9 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.2, K1)*
**QA** is process-oriented (giving confidence the right processes are followed), while **QC** includes product-oriented activities such as **testing**. They are related but distinct.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
Reviewing a design document is static testing that **prevents and finds defects early**, before they become coded faults.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
The **absence-of-errors fallacy** warns that even a defect-free product can fail to meet users' actual needs and expectations.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
A **defect** is a **flaw in a component or system** that can cause it to fail. The human mistake is an error; the observed behavior is a failure.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.2, K2)*
The test process is **context-dependent** and should be **tailored**, matching rigor to each project's risk and circumstances.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Defect reports** are produced during **test execution** (and the analysis of results), when failures are observed.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
**Test design** transforms test conditions into test cases and identifies the test data needed.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.3, K2)*
A **benefit** of independence is that independent testers **question assumptions** and spot defects authors overlook. They complement, not replace, developer responsibility for quality.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
A core good practice: **every development activity has a corresponding test activity**, so testing is integrated throughout.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
In **ATDD**, acceptance tests are derived from the **acceptance criteria** agreed with stakeholders, before development.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.1, K2)*
In **Agile**, testing is **continuous within each iteration**, including regression testing of previously delivered increments.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.3, K2)*
Running a broad suite to catch unintended side effects of changes is **regression testing**. Confirmation testing re-checks a specific fix.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
A response-time requirement is a **performance (non-functional)** requirement, evaluated by **non-functional testing**.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Alpha and beta testing** are forms of **acceptance testing** (alpha at the developer's site, beta at the customer's/user's site).

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
Static testing examines **requirements, models, code, and other readable artifacts** — without executing them.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.1, K1)*
**Frequent feedback** detects and corrects **misunderstandings early**, before they become expensive to fix.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.4, K2)*
A **technical review** focuses on **technical decisions/consensus**, is often led by trained moderators, and involves technical experts/peers.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.3, K2)*
**Static testing** examines work products **without execution**; **dynamic testing** **executes** the software. (Option A reverses this.)

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
Exercising all decisions in the code requires a **white-box** technique such as **branch testing**.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
**Exploratory testing** can **quickly find important defects** and adapt to discoveries, which is valuable when documentation is limited. It does not guarantee full coverage.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** complements systematic techniques by **targeting likely problem areas** they might overlook, based on experience.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.3, K2)*
Checklist-based testing depends on the **relevance and completeness of the checklist** and the tester's judgment in applying it.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
"**Confirmation**" is the **acceptance criteria/tests** that confirm a story is complete — the third C (Card, Conversation, Confirmation).

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.3, K3)*
Automated acceptance tests act as **living documentation** and give **fast feedback** that the acceptance criteria are still met as code evolves.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: C  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
There are **3 valid partitions**, one per pricing tier: 0–2 hours, >2–5 hours, and >5 hours.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
The boundary is between 2 (top of tier 1) and 3 (start of tier 2). **Two-value BVA** tests the boundary value and its nearest neighbor: **2 and 3**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
Two binary conditions give 2² = **4 rules**, each a **unique combination** of the conditions' true/false values, ensuring all combinations are considered.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
A test case is a path that **exercises one or more valid transitions**; for 0-switch coverage, the set of test cases must collectively cover **all valid transitions**. Covering transition *pairs* is 1-switch coverage.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.1, K3)*
With a < b always true here (1 < 2), a **single** test case executes statements 1–6 (including statement 4), achieving 100% statement coverage.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.1, K1)*
The **percentage of planned test cases executed** is a **test-progress** metric. Defect density is a product-quality metric; satisfaction and requirement counts are not progress metrics.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.4.1, K2)*
Without **configuration management**, you cannot reliably **reproduce results**, because the versions of the test objects and testware are not tracked.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
The **risk section** of a test plan documents the **product and project risks** relevant to testing and how they will be addressed.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.4, K2)*
Testing **depth and rigor should be proportional to risk**, concentrating effort where the impact of failure is greatest.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
A database that **may not scale** is a **product risk** — the product itself may fail to meet a (performance/scalability) need.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.2, K2)*
Reports are **tailored to the audience**: executives need summaries and risk/decision focus, while the technical team needs detail.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*

### ✔️ Q37 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.1, K3)*
Risk level = likelihood × impact = 3 × 4 = **12**.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
Irreproducibility usually means the report lacks **clear, complete steps to reproduce** (with test data and environment) — the ID, severity, and reporter do not enable reproduction.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
A **code-coverage** tool supports **white-box / test execution** activities by showing which code the tests exercised.

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: B  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
Realistically, automation **excels at fast, repeatable regression** but **requires investment** to build and maintain — it does not find all defects, replace test design, or guarantee correctness.
