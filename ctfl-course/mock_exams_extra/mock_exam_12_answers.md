# 📝 Mock Exam 12 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.5.2, K1)*
The **whole-team approach** is a hallmark of **Agile/collaborative** development, where the whole team shares responsibility for quality.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
A key objective is to **provide information about quality and risk** so stakeholders can make informed decisions.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
"**Early testing saves time and money**" underpins the **shift-left** idea: because the cost of fixing defects rises the later they are found, finding them early is cheaper.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
The chain is **error → defect → failure**: a human error introduces a defect, which may cause a failure when executed.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.2, K2)*
The volume of test documentation is **context-driven** — regulatory needs, risk level, and the SDLC all influence it.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
Identifying features to test, defining test conditions, and identifying test data are parts of **test analysis and design**.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
Poor **traceability** makes it hard to know **coverage** and **what to retest** after changes, weakening impact analysis.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.3, K2)*
Having **another team member test someone else's work** introduces a degree of independence without a fully separate test team.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
A good practice is that **test objectives are tailored per level** and **testers are involved early** — not identical objectives, skipped regression, or no reviews.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
The red-green-refactor cycle describes **TDD**.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.6, K2)*
**Retrospectives** occur at the **end of an iteration/milestone** to reflect on what went well and identify improvements — not just once at project end.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Acceptance testing** aims to **establish confidence and validate readiness** against business needs, rather than to maximize code-level defect finding.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
**Portability and maintainability** are **non-functional (quality) characteristics**.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.3.1, K2)*
The scope of **maintenance testing** is guided by **impact analysis** — the size/risk of the change and of the existing system.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
A **real-time performance measurement of the running system** requires execution (dynamic testing); it is not a static-testing target. The others are static artifacts.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.2.1, K1)*
Early feedback on a prototype/demo helps **confirm the team is building the right thing**, reducing the risk of wasted effort.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.4, K2)*
An **inspection** is the **most formal** review: defined roles, formal entry/exit criteria, mandatory preparation, and metrics.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.3, K2)*
**Static testing** examines work products to find defects **directly, without execution**, and *can* find requirement defects.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
Using the **specification** (not the code) to derive tests is **black-box** testing.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.3, K2)*
**Checklist-based testing** captures **lessons learned** and ensures **consistent attention** to important items; it does not guarantee coverage or replace other techniques.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
Unstructured exploratory testing risks **uneven, hard-to-reproduce coverage**; charters and notes mitigate this.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** produces a **list of likely failure scenarios** (null, boundaries, concurrency, etc.) to test, based on experience.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.3, K3)*
**ATDD** is collaborative: **business, development, and testing** define the acceptance tests together before development.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
In **INVEST**, "**T**" stands for **Testable** — the story must be verifiable.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: C  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
Valid grades are A, B, C, D, F. The value "**Z**" is not valid, so it belongs to the **invalid** partition.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Boundary value analysis** applies to **ordered/numeric ranges with boundaries**, not to unordered categories or single Booleans.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
In a decision table, **columns are rules** — each a combination of condition outcomes and the resulting actions.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
A **state table** lists **all state/event combinations**, making **invalid/undefined transitions explicit** so they can be tested.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.2, K3)*
A single decision with TRUE and FALSE outcomes needs **2** test cases for full branch (decision) coverage.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.4, K1)*
Estimating from **historical data** of similar past projects is a **metrics-based** estimation technique.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
**Test planning is iterative**: the plan is **refined** as the team learns more; it includes risk and applies to all SDLCs.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.3, K2)*
**Exit criteria** define, with **objective conditions**, when enough testing has been done to **stop**.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.4, K2)*
**Risk-based testing** drives the **selection, prioritization, and depth** of tests, focusing effort where risk is highest.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
A defect in **tax calculation** causing wrong invoices is a **product risk** — the product may fail to meet a need.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.2, K2)*
**Progress reports** are produced **during** testing; **completion reports** are produced at the **end** of a test activity.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*
Highest first: **TD (8)**, then the two **5s** in original order (**TB, TC**), then **TA (2)** → TD, TB, TC, TA.

### ✔️ Q37 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.1, K3)*
Risk level = likelihood × impact = 4 × 1 = **4**.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
A defect report's main goal is to **communicate the problem clearly** so it can be understood, prioritized, and fixed — not to assign blame or hide issues.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
A **unit test framework** is a **test execution/automation** tool used by developers to run tests.

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: B  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
Automation choices should weigh **value, stability, execution frequency, and maintenance cost** — not automate everything blindly or at random.
