# 📝 Mock Exam 11 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
A review's objective is to **find defects early** in the work product. Reviews neither compile nor deploy, and increasing the defect count is not a goal.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.2, K1)*
**Testing** is a form of **quality control** (product-oriented) within overall **quality management**, which also includes process-oriented quality assurance.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
"**Exhaustive testing is impossible**" — since you cannot test every combination, you must **prioritize** using risk and other criteria.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
A **failure** is the observable wrong behavior when defective code is **executed** (the incorrect total shown to the user). The others are defects or an error, not failures.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
Evaluating **exit criteria** against progress is part of **test monitoring and control**.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Testware** includes test cases, scripts, data, and reports — the work products created for and by testing. The application binary is the test object, not testware.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.5, K2)*
In small Agile teams, **developers and testers collaborate**, sharing testing responsibility (the whole-team approach), rather than relying on a separate department.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
**Traceability** reveals coverage and supports **impact analysis** when requirements change — making testing more transparent, not more ambiguous.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
**BDD** uses **business-readable scenarios** (Given/When/Then) to specify behavior and encourage collaboration; it is not a white-box technique.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
A good practice is **continuous tester collaboration with stakeholders** throughout the project, not late or skipped involvement.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.5, K2)*
**Shift-left** is supported by **early reviews, static analysis, and writing tests before code** — moving quality activities earlier.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
**Black-box** testing checks behavior against the specification; **white-box** testing checks the internal structure. (Option C reverses the code-access requirement.)

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**System integration testing** verifies the interactions between the system and **other systems/services**, including their interfaces.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.3, K2)*
**Confirmation testing** re-runs the **same test that failed** to confirm the fix resolves the defect.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.3, K1)*
The **facilitator/moderator** schedules and oversees the review's logistics and ensures the process is followed effectively.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.5, K1)*
Success factors include **clear objectives, trained participants, and management support**. Oversized reviews and blame undermine effectiveness.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.2, K2)*
**Static testing** finds defects **early**, improving quality and reducing cost — it does not require a running system and reduces (not increases) rework.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.2, K2)*
**Planning** sets objectives, selects participants, and defines entry criteria (and scope) for the review.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
**Decision table testing** is a **black-box** technique. Statement testing, branch testing, and code path analysis are white-box.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
White-box testing **reveals structural issues** and lets you **measure code coverage**; it complements (not replaces) black-box testing and does not validate business needs.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
A **test charter** states the **mission/focus** of the exploratory session and may list areas, risks, or test ideas — without scripting exact steps.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** is most effective with **experienced** testers who know the system type and its common defects.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.2, K2)*
Scenario-oriented criteria give **concrete Given/When/Then examples** that translate directly into tests.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
"**Negotiable**" (from INVEST) means the story's details are **open to discussion and refinement**, not fixed or contractual.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: C  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
The valid range is 1–999, so **0** lies in an **invalid** partition. 1, 500, and 999 are all valid.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Two-value BVA** at the upper boundary 999 tests the boundary and its nearest neighbor outside: **999 and 1000**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: D  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
With 4 independent binary conditions, the full decision table has 2⁴ = **16** rules.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
There are **3 valid transitions** (Off→Low, Low→High, High→Off); 0-switch coverage requires exercising all **3**.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.3.1, K3)*
A single test with n = 1 enters the loop (executing statements 4 and 5) and then exits, so **1** test case executes all statements 1–7.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.6, K1)*
The **top** of the pyramid holds the **fewest** tests — the slow, expensive **end-to-end / UI** tests.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.7, K2)*
**Unit and component tests** are technology-facing tests that **support the team** (guide development) — one of the four testing quadrants.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
The **scope** section states **what is and is not included** in the testing effort.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.3, K2)*
For a **low-risk** feature, apply **lighter testing** and shift effort to higher-risk areas — risk-based allocation of effort.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
Undocumented error codes that could make the product **misbehave** represent a **product risk**.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.3, K2)*
A dashboard **communicates test status visually** to stakeholders, supporting transparency and decisions.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*
Highest first: **T3 (9)**, then the two **6s** in original order (**T1, T2**), then **T4 (1)** → T3, T1, T2, T4.

### ✔️ Q37 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.2.1, K3)*
Risk level = likelihood × impact = 1 × 1 = **1**.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
**Critical** severity fits a **crash with data loss** — a major impact on the system. Cosmetic issues warrant low severity.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
A **test management tool** maintains requirements-to-test **traceability** (and manages cases, runs, and results).

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: B  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
Automation in CI provides **rapid, automatic feedback** on whether a change broke existing functionality — it does not remove the need for tests or guarantee perfection.
