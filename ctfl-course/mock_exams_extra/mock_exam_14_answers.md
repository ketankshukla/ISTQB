# 📝 Mock Exam 14 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
A test objective in operation can be to **assess characteristics like reliability or availability** under realistic conditions — not to hide failures or stop monitoring.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.5.1, K1)*
**Critical/analytical thinking** helps a tester scrutinize requirements for gaps, ambiguities, and contradictions.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
The **absence-of-errors fallacy** warns that fixing all known bugs does not guarantee a useful product if it fails to meet real user needs.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
A human action producing an incorrect result is an **error (mistake)**, which may introduce a defect.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
**Traceability** lets you report **coverage** meaningfully, e.g., the percentage of requirements with passing tests.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
**Test design** follows test analysis: conditions identified in analysis are turned into test cases during design.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Test implementation** produces **test procedures/suites and test data**, readying everything for execution.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.2, K2)*
The test process is **context-dependent**: differing risk, regulation, skills, and tools lead teams to adopt different processes.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
A good practice across SDLCs is that **every development activity has a corresponding test activity**.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
In **ATDD**, the "A" stands for **Acceptance** (Acceptance Test-Driven Development).

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.4, K2)*
**Continuous deployment** depends on **robust automated tests** to catch regressions quickly before changes reach production.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Component (unit) testing** is the lowest test level, testing individual units/components.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
Rejecting an invalid date format checks a **function** against requirements — **functional testing**.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.3.1, K2)*
After a hotfix, perform **confirmation testing** of the fix and **regression testing** of impacted areas — typical maintenance testing.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.3, K1)*
The **reviewer** identifies **potential defects/issues**; fixing belongs to the author and leading to the facilitator.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.1, K1)*
**Early feedback** reduces the **cost of change**, because issues are caught before they propagate.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.4, K2)*
A **walkthrough** is led by the author to educate participants and gather feedback, with **moderate formality**.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.2, K2)*
Statically reviewing requirements improves their **clarity, consistency, and completeness**, preventing downstream defects.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
**White-box** techniques derive tests from the **internal structure** (statements, branches, paths).

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
Coverage measures show **how thoroughly the code structure has been exercised** — a structural, not requirements or usability, measure.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** targets likely fault triggers: **zero, negatives, very large values, and special characters**.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.3, K2)*
**Checklist-based testing** lists items/conditions to verify while leaving the exact steps to the tester's judgment.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
"**Independent**" (INVEST) means a story is **self-contained** enough to be built and tested on its own where possible.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.5.3, K3)*
In **ATDD**, the agreed examples become the **basis for the acceptance tests** that confirm the criteria.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: D  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
Valid hours are 0–23, so **24** is in the **invalid** partition.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Two-value BVA** at the lower boundary 0 tests the boundary and its nearest neighbor outside: **-1 and 0**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
When **both** username and password are invalid, the expected action is to **deny access** (login fails).

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
There are **4 valid transitions** (insert card, correct PIN, wrong PIN, eject); 0-switch coverage requires all **4**.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: C  *(FL-4.3.2, K3)*
Three test cases cover all branch outcomes: **temp = 150** (hot), **temp = -5** (cold), **temp = 50** (normal) — exercising both decisions' true and false outcomes.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.2, K1)*
A tester adds value to release planning by providing input on **test effort, risks, and feasibility** of delivering quality.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
A test plan includes **deliverables and milestones** (plus scope, approach, schedule, risks, criteria) — not HR or marketing data.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.3, K2)*
**Entry criteria** include a **stable test basis and an available environment** — preconditions to start. Passing tests and signed reports are exit criteria.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.4, K2)*
**Higher product risk** calls for **more rigorous, thorough testing** of that area.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
Possible **performance degradation** under load is a **product risk** — the product may fail to meet a need.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.2, K2)*
Report content is **tailored to purpose and audience** — detailed for the team, summarized for executives.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*
Highest first: the two **9s** in original order (**TC, TD**), then **TA (7)**, then **TB (3)** → TC, TD, TA, TB.

### ✔️ Q37 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.1, K3)*
Risk level = likelihood × impact = 3 × 5 = **15**.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
Recording the **build/version** matters because a defect may be **version-specific**, aiding reproduction and the fix.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
Tools that design or generate test data fall under **test design / test data preparation tools**.

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: D  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
Automation does **not** eliminate maintenance — keeping tests up to date is an ongoing cost. The other options are genuine benefits.
