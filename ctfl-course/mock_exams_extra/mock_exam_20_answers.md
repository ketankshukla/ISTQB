# 📝 Mock Exam 20 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
**Preventing defects** through early review involvement is a legitimate test objective — the others contradict testing's purpose.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.2, K1)*
**QA is process-oriented**; **QC is product-oriented and includes testing**.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
**Defects cluster together**: concentrating extra testing on the high-defect modules is the appropriate response.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
The chain is **error → defect → failure**; not every error becomes a failure, and defects are distinct from failures.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
Comparing **actual vs expected** results is part of **test execution**.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Test monitoring and control** produces **progress reports and control directives**.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.4, K2)*
Traceability supports **coverage assessment and impact analysis** between the test basis and test cases.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.2, K2)*
In the **whole-team approach**, the **whole team collaborates** to resolve quality issues.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
**Testers reviewing requirements** is a good practice in any SDLC — early feedback prevents defects.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
In **TDD**, tests are written **before the code**, guiding its design (red-green-refactor).

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.5, K2)*
Reviewing **user stories and acceptance criteria before development** shifts testing **left** (earlier).

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Component testing** focuses on **individual units in isolation**.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
Verifying correct report totals tests **what the system does** — **functional testing**.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.3.1, K2)*
Adding a **new feature to a live, released product** triggers **maintenance testing**; the others are initial development or project events.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.3, K1)*
The **facilitator/moderator** ensures the review runs **effectively** and mediates disagreements.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
A **peer review of a design document** is **static testing** (no execution). The others involve execution/measurement.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.2, K2)*
Static testing can find defects (e.g., inconsistent requirements, unreachable code) that are **hard/impossible to detect dynamically**; it complements, not replaces, dynamic testing.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.4, K2)*
A **walkthrough** is **often led by the author** and is useful for **knowledge sharing and finding defects**, at moderate formality.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
With only the specification and no code, you use **black-box** techniques.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
Combining approaches gives both **requirements-based (black-box)** and **structure-based (white-box)** assurance.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
Time-boxed, charter-driven exploratory testing is **session-based test management**.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** is an **experience-based** technique anticipating likely mistakes and defects.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
"**Confirmation**" is the **acceptance criteria/tests** that confirm the story is done.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.3, K3)*
A key ATDD outcome is **concrete, shared acceptance tests** that guide development and reduce ambiguity.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: D  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
When a condition does not affect an outcome, its cell is marked **"don't care" (-)**.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
Three valid transitions (Green→Yellow, Yellow→Red, Red→Green) → 0-switch coverage needs all **3**.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: C  *(FL-4.3.2, K3)*

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.6, K1)*
The pyramid concentrates the **most automated tests at the unit/component level**.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.2, K2)*
A tester aids planning by **estimating test effort** and **identifying risks and dependencies**.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
A test plan's purpose is to **guide and control testing** and **communicate it to stakeholders**.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.2.3, K2)*
Product risk analysis informs the **depth and priority of testing** for each area.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
A **crash under peak load** is a **product risk**; understaffing, schedule slip, and budget cuts are project risks.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.2, K2)*
A **completion/summary report** covers **what was tested, results, deviations, residual risks, and lessons learned**.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
