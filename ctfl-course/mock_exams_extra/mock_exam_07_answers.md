# 📝 Mock Exam 7 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.2, K1)*
The **whole-team approach** increases **shared ownership of quality** and improves knowledge sharing, because the whole team collaborates on quality rather than delegating it to testers alone.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.1.1, K1)*
**Gaining confidence** that the system meets its requirements is a typical test objective. Testing never aims to leave defects undiscovered, avoid communication, or prove absence of defects.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
The **pesticide paradox**: repeating the same tests stops finding new defects over time, so tests must be reviewed and updated.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
A **defect** is a flaw in a work product (e.g., code); a **failure** is the observable wrong behavior when that defective code is executed.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Test execution** produces **test logs, actual results, and defect reports**. The plan and strategy come earlier; requirements are part of the test basis.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
The test process flows: **planning → analysis → design → implementation → execution → completion** (with monitoring and control throughout).

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.1, K2)*
Testing is necessary because **humans make errors** and complex systems can fail unexpectedly; testing reduces the risk of operational failures.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.3, K2)*
A **drawback** of strong isolation is **communication gaps and slower feedback**. Objectivity and better defect detection are *benefits* of independence.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
Good practice: **each test level has clear objectives** and **testers are involved early**, ensuring appropriate coverage and early defect detection.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
**TDD** is a test-first approach. Waterfall and big-bang integration are not test-first, and ad-hoc testing is not a development approach.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.6, K2)*
A **retrospective** produces **improvement actions** (like adding earlier automated checks). It is about improvement, not blame, and does not produce requirements or architecture.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.2.3, K2)*
**Confirmation testing** verifies a fix works; **regression testing** checks that changes have not caused **unintended side effects** elsewhere.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
Reliability, performance, and usability are **non-functional (quality) characteristics**, evaluated by non-functional testing.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Acceptance testing** is typically the final level before release and validates the system against **business needs** and readiness for use.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
A **requirements specification** is a readable work product suitable for static testing. Running binaries, live traffic, and CPU temperature are dynamic/operational concerns.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.5, K1)*
Success factors include **adequate time, the right participants, and a constructive atmosphere**. Vague objectives, oversized sessions, and skipping reporting all undermine reviews.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.4, K2)*
A **technical review** uses a **documented process**, focuses on **technical decisions**, is ideally led by a trained moderator, and involves peers/technical experts.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.3, K2)*
Static and dynamic testing are **complementary**: static finds defects directly in work products, while dynamic finds failures by executing the software.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
Deriving tests from a **behavioral model**, ignoring implementation, is a **black-box** technique. Statement and branch testing are white-box.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
High **branch coverage** indicates that most **decision outcomes** in the code have been exercised — a structural (white-box) confidence measure, not a statement about requirements or usability.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** is an **experience-based** technique, relying on the tester's knowledge of likely defects.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.2, K2)*
A session **debrief** reviews **what was tested, findings, issues, and charter coverage**, feeding into planning the next sessions.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.3, K3)*
**ATDD** builds a **shared understanding** and produces **concrete, testable examples before development**, which then guide and confirm the implementation.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
Collaborative story writing draws on **business, development, and testing** perspectives (the "Three Amigos") to create well-understood, testable stories.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
**25 and 75** are both within the valid 0–100 partition. The other pairs mix valid with invalid values (or both invalid).

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Three-value BVA** at the lower boundary 0 tests the boundary and both neighbors: **-1, 0, 1**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
One binary condition gives 2¹ = **2** rules (true and false).

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
The cycle has **3 transitions** (Red→Green, Green→Yellow, Yellow→Red); 0-switch coverage requires exercising all **3**.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.1, K3)*
Straight-line code with **no decisions** is fully executed by **a single test case**, achieving 100% statement coverage regardless of how many statements there are.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.1, K1)*
**Defect density** is a **product-quality** metric. Test cases per day and hours/meetings are progress/effort metrics, not product quality.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
The **approach** section explains **how** testing will be done — levels, types, techniques, environments, and tools — not defect lists, budgets, or code.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.3, K2)*
**Entry criteria** prevent starting an activity prematurely (e.g., before the environment is ready), avoiding wasted effort. They do not guarantee zero defects or replace exit criteria.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.4, K2)*
With limited time, **focus on the highest-risk areas first** so the most important risks are addressed before time runs out.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
An algorithm that may produce **incorrect results** is a **product risk** — a risk that the product itself fails to meet needs.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.2, K2)*
A **test completion report** summarizes **what was tested, results, deviations, residual risks, and lessons learned** for stakeholders at the end of the activity.

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*
Highest business value first: R1 (high), R3 (medium), R2 (low) → **R1, R3, R2**.

### ✔️ Q37 — Answer: C  *(FL-5.2.1, K3)*
Risk level = likelihood × impact. X = 1 × 5 = 5; Y = 5 × 1 = 5. They are **equal** (both 5), though their treatment may differ.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
Reproducibility needs **preconditions, step-by-step actions, test data, and observed vs expected results**. A bare summary, severity, or date is not enough.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: B  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
A **defect/test management tool** records, tracks, and manages defects and/or test cases and runs — it does not generate load, compile code, or measure temperature.

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: B  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
A **risk** of automation is **false confidence** when tests are outdated or verify the wrong things. Speed, repeatability, and reduced manual effort are benefits.
