# 📝 Mock Exam 18 — Answer Key (Extended Practice Set)

Each answer shows the **correct option**, a **rationale**, the **Learning Objective (LO)** it tests, and the **K-level**.

---

### ✔️ Q1 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.1, K1)*
Testing is necessary because it **reduces the risk of operational failures**, contributing to success — it cannot guarantee perfection or replace development.

### ✔️ Q2 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.2.2, K1)*
**Testing the product to find defects** is a **quality control** (product-oriented) activity. Defining/auditing processes belongs to quality assurance.

### ✔️ Q3 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.3.1, K2)*
The **seven principles** provide **general guidance** to help testers avoid common mistakes — not guarantees or replacements for techniques.

### ✔️ Q4 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.2.3, K2)*
The full chain is **root cause → error → defect → failure → impact**.

### ✔️ Q5 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.3, K2)*
**Test cases** are outputs of **test design**; source code, requirements, and deployment scripts are not test-design outputs.

### ✔️ Q6 — Answer: A  *(FL-1.4.1, K2)*
Prioritizing test cases and building **suites/procedures** occurs during **test implementation**.

### ✔️ Q7 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.4.5, K2)*
**Organizing/coordinating testing and reporting overall status** is a **test management** task; executing/logging individual tests is the testing role.

### ✔️ Q8 — Answer: B  *(FL-1.5.3, K2)*
Independence brings **different perspectives**, helping to recognize **different kinds of failures**.

### ✔️ Q9 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.2, K2)*
Good testing is **adapted to the SDLC and context**, not identical everywhere or unplanned.

### ✔️ Q10 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.1.3, K1)*
**BDD** scenarios use the **Given / When / Then** structure.

### ✔️ Q11 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.1.6, K2)*
A **retrospective** identifies **what went well and what to improve** — a blame-free improvement activity.

### ✔️ Q12 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.1, K2)*
**Contract acceptance testing** checks the system against the **acceptance criteria in a contract**.

### ✔️ Q13 — Answer: B  *(FL-2.2.2, K2)*
**Reliability and security** are **non-functional quality characteristics**.

### ✔️ Q14 — Answer: A  *(FL-2.3.1, K2)*
**Impact analysis** identifies the **areas affected by a change** so the right regression tests are selected.

### ✔️ Q15 — Answer: A  *(FL-3.2.3, K1)*
The **author** creates and is responsible for the **work product** under review.

### ✔️ Q16 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.1.1, K1)*
**Static testing** can be applied to **non-executable work products** like requirements, and can be done manually or with tools.

### ✔️ Q17 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.4, K2)*
The **inspection** is the most formal review, with defined roles, entry/exit criteria, and metrics.

### ✔️ Q18 — Answer: B  *(FL-3.2.1, K2)*
Stakeholder involvement builds **common understanding** and detects **deviations from expectations early**.

### ✔️ Q19 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.1.1, K2)*
**Black-box** techniques analyze **specified behavior** without reference to internal structure.

### ✔️ Q20 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.3, K2)*
Code coverage **identifies structural elements not yet exercised**; it does not prove requirements or absence of defects.

### ✔️ Q21 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.4.1, K2)*
**Error guessing** relies on the tester's **knowledge and experience** of likely defects.

### ✔️ Q22 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.4.3, K2)*
Checklist-based testing can **miss items not listed** and **become stale** if the checklist is not maintained.

### ✔️ Q23 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.3, K3)*
In **ATDD**, acceptance tests are defined **before or during development, collaboratively** — driving the implementation.

### ✔️ Q24 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.5.1, K2)*
"**Small**" (INVEST) means the story is **small enough to complete within an iteration**.

### ✔️ Q25 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.1, K3)*
Valid lengths are 8–16, so **12** is a valid representative. 5 and 0 are too short; 20 is too long.

### ✔️ Q26 — Answer: A  *(FL-4.2.2, K3)*
**Three-value BVA** at the lower boundary 8 tests **7, 8, 9**.

### ✔️ Q27 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.3, K3)*
A **dash "-"** marks a **"don't care"** — the condition's value does not affect that rule's outcome.

### ✔️ Q28 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.2.4, K3)*
There are **2 valid transitions** (coin, push); 0-switch coverage requires both.

### ✔️ Q29 — Answer: B  *(FL-4.3.2, K3)*
**Decision coverage** treats the whole condition as one decision; you need it to be **true** (a=T, b=T) and **false** (e.g., a=F) → **2** test cases.

### ✔️ Q30 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.4, K1)*
Estimation is influenced by **product characteristics** such as complexity and the required quality level.

### ✔️ Q31 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.1, K2)*
The **schedule** section defines **when** test activities occur and their **milestones**.

### ✔️ Q32 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.3, K2)*
**Entry/exit criteria** make **start/stop decisions objective and consistent**.

### ✔️ Q33 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.2.3, K2)*
Product risk analysis produces a **ranked list of risks** that guides where to focus testing.

### ✔️ Q34 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.2.2, K2)*
Potential **data loss from a backup failure** is a **product risk** — the product may fail to meet a need.

### ✔️ Q35 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.3.2, K2)*
Reporting frequency should suit **stakeholder needs and the project's cadence** (e.g., each iteration).

### ✔️ Q36 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.1.5, K3)*
Highest first: **R3 (9)**, then the two **5s** in original order (**R1, R4**), then **R2 (1)** → R3, R1, R4, R2.

### ✔️ Q37 — Answer: B  *(FL-5.2.1, K3)*
Risk level = likelihood × impact = 1 × 5 = **5**.

### ✔️ Q38 — Answer: A  *(FL-5.5.1, K3)*
A **cosmetic (low-severity)** issue can still be **high-priority** if it is highly visible (e.g., on the CEO's demo) and must be fixed quickly.

### ✔️ Q39 — Answer: A  *(FL-6.1.1, K1)*
**CI tools** automatically **build and test the code on each change**.

### ✔️ Q40 — Answer: B  *(FL-6.2.1, K2)*
A good practice is a **pilot project** to evaluate a tool's fit before wider adoption, with proper training and attention to maintenance.
