# CT-FT Glossary

## A

- **Accrual** — Revenue or expense recognized before cash is exchanged
- **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)** — Regulations and processes to prevent money laundering
- **AP (Accounts Payable)** — Money owed by a company to its creditors
- **AR (Accounts Receivable)** — Money owed to a company by its customers

## B

- **Basel III** — International regulatory framework for bank capital adequacy
- **Batch Processing** — Processing transactions in groups at scheduled times
- **Bid-Ask Spread** — Difference between the highest price a buyer will pay and the lowest price a seller will accept

## C

- **CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model)** — Formula for expected return on an asset
- **CD (Certificate of Deposit)** — Time deposit with a fixed term and interest rate
- **Compliance Testing** — Testing to verify adherence to laws, regulations, and standards
- **Core Banking System** — Central system processing daily banking transactions and account updates

## D

- **Derivative** — Financial contract whose value depends on an underlying asset
- **Discount Rate** — Interest rate used to calculate present value of future cash flows

## E

- **EMIR (European Market Infrastructure Regulation)** — EU regulation for OTC derivatives
- **Equity** — Ownership interest in a company (shares/stock)

## F

- **FIX Protocol** — Financial Information eXchange standard for electronic trading
- **FPGA (Fixed-Price Guarantee Arrangement)** — Not applicable; see floating vs fixed rate
- **FX (Foreign Exchange)** — Currency trading market

## G

- **GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)** — US accounting standards
- **GDPR** — General Data Protection Regulation (affects financial data handling)

## H

- **Hedge** — Investment to reduce risk of adverse price movements

## I

- **IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards)** — Global accounting standards
- **Interest Rate Risk** — Risk that interest rate changes affect asset values
- **IPO (Initial Public Offering)** — First sale of stock to the public

## K

- **KYC (Know Your Customer)** — Process of verifying customer identity

## L

- **Ledger** — Book of accounts containing all financial transactions
- **LIBOR/SOFR** — Benchmark interest rates (LIBOR is being replaced by SOFR)

## M

- **MiFID II** — EU regulation for financial markets and investment services
- **MTM (Mark-to-Market)** — Valuing assets at current market prices

## N

- **NAV (Net Asset Value)** — Value of a fund's assets minus liabilities
- **NIST** — US standards body; relevant for financial cybersecurity frameworks

## O

- **OTC (Over-the-Counter)** — Trading directly between parties without an exchange

## P

- **PCI-DSS** — Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard
- **Precision** — Number of decimal places in a calculation
- **Present Value (PV)** — Current value of a future sum of money

## R

- **Reconciliation** — Process of matching records between two systems
- **Regulatory Reporting** — Mandatory reports submitted to regulators
- **Risk-Based Testing** — Prioritizing tests based on risk of financial loss
- **ROI (Return on Investment)** — Measure of investment profitability
- **Round-Trip Testing** — Testing a full transaction cycle (initiation to settlement)

## S

- **Settlement** — Final transfer of securities/funds after a trade
- **SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)** — US law regulating financial reporting and internal controls
- **SWIFT** — Secure messaging network for international payments

## T

- **T+1 / T+2** — Settlement cycle (trade date + 1 or 2 business days)
- **Transaction Processing** — Handling individual financial transactions in real time
- **Treasury** — Department managing cash, investments, and financial risk

## V

- **VaR (Value at Risk)** — Statistical measure of potential portfolio loss
- **Volatility** — Measure of price variation over time

## Y

- **Yield** — Income return on an investment
