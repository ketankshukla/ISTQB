# Chapter 3 — Regulatory Compliance and Risk Management: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Confusing SOX with GDPR

**Trap:** SOX = financial reporting controls. GDPR = personal data protection. Both affect finance but for different reasons.

**Example question:**
> A bank implements controls to prevent unauthorized changes to financial statements. This is required by:
>
> A. GDPR
> B. SOX
> C. PCI-DSS
> D. MiFID II

**Correct answer:** B — SOX Section 404 requires internal controls over financial reporting.

---

## Trap 2: Thinking MiFID II Only Applies to the UK

**Trap:** MiFID II applies to EU investment firms and trading venues, not just the UK. Post-Brexit, the UK has its own MiFID-style rules.

**Example question:**
> MiFID II transaction reporting requirements apply to:
>
> A. Only UK banks
> B. EU investment firms and trading venues
> C. Only US broker-dealers
> D. All global banks regardless of location

**Correct answer:** B — MiFID II is an EU regulation.

---

## Trap 3: Confusing KYC with AML

**Trap:** KYC = verifying customer identity at onboarding. AML = ongoing monitoring for suspicious activity. Different but related processes.

**Example question:**
> Screening a customer against sanctions lists during account opening is part of:
>
> A. AML monitoring
> B. KYC verification
> C. PCI-DSS compliance
> D. Basel III reporting

**Correct answer:** B — Sanctions screening during onboarding is KYC.

---

## Trap 4: Thinking Regulatory Testing Is a One-Time Activity

**Trap:** Regulations change, systems change, and controls must be tested continuously. Annual SOX testing is required.

**Example question:**
> A bank tested all SOX controls at go-live two years ago and found them effective. The current audit risk is:
>
> A. Zero because controls were effective at go-live
> B. High because SOX requires ongoing testing and changes may have introduced gaps
> C. Low because controls don't change
> D. Only relevant for new systems

**Correct answer:** B — SOX requires ongoing testing; system changes can introduce control gaps.

---

## Trap 5: Confusing Market Risk with Credit Risk

**Trap:** Market risk = loss from price movements. Credit risk = loss from borrower default. Different calculations and controls.

**Example question:**
> A test validates that a bond portfolio's Value-at-Risk (VaR) is calculated correctly. This tests:
>
> A. Credit risk
> B. Market risk
> C. Operational risk
> D. Liquidity risk

**Correct answer:** B — VaR measures potential loss from market price movements.
