# Chapter 5 — Evaluating and Deploying MBT: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Ignoring Model Maintenance Costs in ROI

**Trap:** ROI calculations often focus on test generation savings but underestimate ongoing model maintenance effort.

**Example question:**
> A team calculates MBT ROI using only initial model creation time and test generation savings. Their ROI will likely be:
>
> A. Accurate
> B. Overestimated because ongoing model maintenance costs are ignored
> C. Underestimated
> D. Only valid for the first year

**Correct answer:** B — Ongoing model maintenance, concretization updates, and tool costs must be included.

---

## Trap 2: Choosing a High-Risk Project for the Pilot

**Trap:** Pilots should be low-risk, well-understood projects. Critical or unstable projects make it hard to distinguish MBT issues from project issues.

**Example question:**
> A team selects a brand-new, unstable project with tight deadlines as their MBT pilot. The problem is:
>
> A. It will prove MBT works faster
> B. It will be difficult to distinguish MBT learning issues from project instability issues
> C. It is the only valid choice
> D. It reduces training costs

**Correct answer:** B — Unstable projects confound pilot results. Choose stable, well-understood projects.

---

## Trap 3: Thinking Big-Bang Is Always Best

**Trap:** Big-bang deployment is high-risk and rarely recommended. Incremental deployment with a pilot is safer.

**Example question:**
> A large organization with 10 test teams wants to adopt MBT. The SAFEST approach is:
>
> A. Big-bang — train all 10 teams simultaneously
> B. Incremental — pilot with 1 team, learn, then expand
> C. Only adopt MBT for the smallest project
> D. Wait until all testers have PhDs

**Correct answer:** B — Incremental deployment with pilot is the safest approach for most organizations.

---

## Trap 4: Thinking MBT Requires Perfect Requirements

**Trap:** While stable requirements help, MBT can work with iterative development. The model can evolve with requirements.

**Example question:**
> A team believes they cannot use MBT because their requirements change frequently. This belief is:
>
> A. Correct — MBT requires frozen requirements
> B. Incorrect — models can be iteratively updated as requirements change, and MBT can even help identify requirement issues early
> C. Only true for agile projects
> D. Required by ISTQB standards

**Correct answer:** B — Models evolve with requirements. MBT actually helps catch requirement problems early.

---

## Trap 5: Confusing Process Maturity and MBT Suitability

**Trap:** Low maturity makes MBT harder but not impossible. MBT can actually help improve maturity.

**Example question:**
> An organization with ad-hoc testing processes believes MBT is only for mature organizations. The truth is:
>
> A. They are correct — MBT requires CMM Level 5
> B. MBT is harder with low maturity but can help improve processes; a pilot with realistic expectations is still viable
> C. They should first achieve perfect processes before any MBT
> D. MBT automatically makes any organization mature

**Correct answer:** B — Low maturity makes it harder, but MBT can help improve processes. Realistic pilot expectations are key.
