# Chapter 2 — Test Automation Planning and ROI: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Expecting Immediate ROI

**Trap:** Automation is a long-term investment. Year 1 typically shows negative ROI due to setup costs.

**Example question:**
> A team calculates negative ROI in the first year of automation and decides to cancel the project. This is:
>
> A. Correct — negative ROI means failure
> B. Incorrect — automation benefits compound over time; break-even is typically 12-24 months
> C. Only wrong for large companies
> D. Only wrong for regulated industries

**Correct answer:** B — Automation is a long-term investment with compounding returns.

---

## Trap 2: Ignoring Maintenance Costs

**Trap:** Maintenance is 30-50% of initial effort annually. Ignoring it makes ROI unrealistic.

**Example question:**
> A business case includes initial tool costs and script development but omits maintenance. The result is:
>
> A. A conservative estimate
> B. An overly optimistic ROI that will not be achieved
> C. Only a missing line item
> D. Only wrong for agile projects

**Correct answer:** B — Maintenance is a significant ongoing cost that must be included.

---

## Trap 3: Automating Low-Value Tests

**Trap:** Not all tests provide equal value. Automating rarely-run tests has poor ROI.

**Example question:**
> A team automates a test that runs once per year and takes 30 minutes manually. The ROI is:
>
> A. Excellent — every automated test is valuable
> B. Poor — the maintenance cost will exceed the tiny time savings
> C. Only good if the tool is free
> D. Only bad for large teams

**Correct answer:** B — Automate frequently-run, high-risk tests for best ROI.

---

## Trap 4: Calculating ROI with Only Time Savings

**Trap:** ROI should include quality improvements (fewer production defects), faster time-to-market, and risk reduction.

**Example question:**
> An ROI calculation only includes manual tester hours saved. It likely:
>
> A. Is complete and accurate
> B. Underestimates total value by ignoring quality improvements, defect prevention, and faster releases
> C. Only misses training costs
> D. Only misses tool costs

**Correct answer:** B — Time savings is just one benefit; quality and speed also matter.

---

## Trap 5: Planning Without Risk Assessment

**Trap:** Every automation project has risks. Not planning for them leads to surprises and failure.

**Example question:**
> A team launches automation without identifying risks. The most likely outcome is:
>
> A. Smooth execution
> B. Unanticipated obstacles cause delays, cost overruns, or cancellation
> C. Only slower test creation
> D. Only more documentation

**Correct answer:** B — Risk assessment and mitigation planning are essential for success.
