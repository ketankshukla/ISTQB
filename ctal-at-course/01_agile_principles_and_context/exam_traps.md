# Chapter 1 — Agile Principles and Context: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Thinking Agile Means No Documentation

**Trap:** Agile values working software over documentation, but some documentation (acceptance criteria, test cases, architectural decisions) is still necessary.

**Example question:**
> A stakeholder criticizes an agile team for having "too little documentation." The team should:
>
> A. Immediately write comprehensive documentation for everything
> B. Explain that agile values working software over comprehensive documentation, but necessary documentation is still produced
> C. Stop all documentation efforts completely
> D. Switch to waterfall development

**Correct answer:** B — Agile does not mean zero documentation. It means producing just enough documentation to support the project.

---

## Trap 2: Confusing Scrum and Kanban

**Trap:** Scrum has fixed sprints and prescribed ceremonies. Kanban has continuous flow and no prescribed ceremonies.

**Example question:**
> A team does not use sprints. Work items are pulled when capacity is available, and WIP limits are enforced. This team is using:
>
> A. Scrum
> B. Kanban
> C. Waterfall
> D. XP

**Correct answer:** B — Continuous flow, pull-based work, and WIP limits are hallmarks of Kanban. Scrum uses fixed sprints.

---

## Trap 3: Thinking Testers Are the Only Ones Responsible for Quality

**Trap:** In agile, quality is a whole-team responsibility. Developers write unit tests, the PO defines acceptance criteria, and everyone participates in quality.

**Example question:**
> In an agile team, who is responsible for ensuring the quality of the product increment?
>
> A. Only the testers
> B. Only the Scrum Master
> C. The entire development team
> D. Only the Product Owner

**Correct answer:** C — The whole team is responsible for quality. Testers play a key role but are not solely responsible.

---

## Trap 4: Confusing the Definition of Done with the Definition of Ready

**Trap:** DoD = criteria for work to be considered complete. DoR = criteria for work to be ready to start.

**Example question:**
> "Acceptance criteria are clear and testable" is part of:
>
> A. The Definition of Done
> B. The Definition of Ready
> C. The Sprint Goal
> D. The Product Vision

**Correct answer:** B — Clear acceptance criteria are needed BEFORE work starts (DoR). Passing acceptance tests is part of DoD.

---

## Trap 5: Thinking Agile Teams Don't Need Test Automation

**Trap:** Agile teams absolutely need test automation to support frequent releases and continuous feedback.

**Example question:**
> An agile team releases every two weeks. They currently rely entirely on manual regression testing. The BIGGEST risk is:
>
> A. The team is following agile correctly
> B. Manual regression testing cannot keep pace with frequent releases
> C. Test automation is not required in agile
> D. The team should extend their sprint length

**Correct answer:** B — Frequent releases require fast feedback. Manual regression testing is too slow for a 2-week release cadence.
