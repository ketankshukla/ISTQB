## Answers and Rationales

### Q1
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** BAT = verify system meets business needs and requirements.

### Q2
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Well-written = specific, measurable, testable.

### Q3
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Given-When-Then = structured BDD format.

### Q4
**Answer: D**

**Rationale:** No workaround, blocks process = critical.

### Q5
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** ATDD = acceptance tests written before development.

### Q6
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Three Amigos = business, developer, tester collaboration.

### Q7
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Pilot = limited production validation before full rollout.

### Q8
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Sign-off = business accepts with known issues and plans.

### Q9
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** UAT data = realistic, production-like, covering edge cases.

### Q10
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Specific, testable, business-focused criterion.

### Q11
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** UAT = business users and representatives participate.

### Q12
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** BDD = business-readable Given-When-Then format.

### Q13
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Canary = small % of users, monitor, then expand.

### Q14
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Defects = logged, prioritized, tracked through resolution.

### Q15
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Acceptance criteria = business-focused, not technical details.

### Q16
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** ATDD = define acceptance criteria and tests before code.

### Q17
**Answer: D**

**Rationale:** Developer's favorite color = not relevant to sign-off.

### Q18
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** UAT environment = mirror production closely.

### Q19
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Rule-based = clear conditions and outcomes for business rules.

### Q20
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Phased rollout = one department/location at a time.
