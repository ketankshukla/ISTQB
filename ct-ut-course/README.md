## How to Use This Course

1. **Start with `STUDY_PLAN.md`** — Pick the 3-week or accelerated schedule
2. **Read chapters in order** — Each chapter builds on the previous
3. **Study `lessons.md` first** — Read the detailed explanations and worked examples
4. **Review `exam_traps.md`** — Learn the common mistakes before you make them
5. **Practice with `practice_questions.md`** — 20 questions per chapter with full rationales
6. **Do the case studies** — Integrated scenarios that span multiple chapters
7. **Take the mock exams** — Simulate exam conditions (40 questions, 60 minutes)
8. **Track progress in `PROGRESS_TRACKER.md`** — Know when you are exam-ready

## Target Audience

- Testers involved in usability testing
- UX researchers and designers working with testers
- QA engineers responsible for user experience validation
- Anyone seeking the ISTQB CT-UT certification

## Key Topics Covered

- **Usability and UX fundamentals** — Definitions, principles, standards (ISO 9241, ISO 25010)
- **Usability testing process** — Planning, preparation, execution, analysis, reporting
- **Evaluation methods** — User-based testing, expert-based review, cognitive walkthroughs, heuristic evaluation
- **Accessibility and inclusive design** — WCAG, assistive technologies, disability categories
- **Tools and metrics** — Questionnaires (SUS, NPS, CSUQ), task metrics, error metrics
- **Organizational aspects** — Roles, responsibilities, integration into the SDLC, business value

## Tip for Maximum Exam Success

> **Do not just read. Practice.** After each chapter, answer the practice questions without looking at the answers. Then read the rationales to understand WHY the correct answer is right and WHY the distractors are wrong. This active recall is the single most effective study technique for multiple-choice exams.
