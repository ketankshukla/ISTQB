# 🪤 Chapter 6 — Organizational Aspects: Exam Traps

## 🪤 Trap 1: Thinking Usability Testing Should Only Happen at the End

**Trap:** Modern practice integrates usability throughout the SDLC (shift-left). Testing only at the end is too late and expensive.

**Example question:**
> A team plans to conduct usability testing only after the product is fully developed. The MAIN risk is:
>
> A. The testing will be more thorough
> B. Fixing usability problems will be more expensive and may require major rework
> C. Users will be more available
> D. The test environment will be more realistic

**Correct answer:** B — Late testing means expensive fixes. Shift-left catches issues early when they are cheaper to fix.

---

## 🪤 Trap 2: Thinking ROI Cannot Be Measured for Usability

**Trap:** Usability ROI can be measured: reduced support costs, increased conversion, reduced training, avoided rework.

**Example question:**
> A stakeholder argues that usability testing has no measurable return on investment. The BEST response is:
>
> A. Agree — usability is a qualitative benefit only
> B. Show metrics like reduced support calls, increased conversion rates, and reduced development rework
> C. Ignore the stakeholder and test anyway
> D. Reduce the scope of testing

**Correct answer:** B — Usability ROI is measurable through support costs, conversion, training time, and rework reduction.

---

## 🪤 Trap 3: Confusing UX Roles

**Trap:** UX researcher conducts tests; usability engineer applies principles; interaction designer creates interfaces; developer implements.

**Example question:**
> A professional who primarily conducts user interviews, facilitates usability tests, and analyzes user behavior data is BEST described as a:
>
> A. Developer
> B. UX Researcher
> C. Product Owner
> D. Test Automation Engineer

**Correct answer:** B — UX researchers plan and conduct user research and usability tests.

---

## 🪤 Trap 4: Thinking Low UX Maturity Is a Permanent State

**Trap:** Organizations can progress through UX maturity levels with effort, sponsorship, and demonstrated value.

**Example question:**
> An organization has no UX budget, no UX roles, and no usability testing. This means:
>
> A. The organization will never improve
> B. The organization is at low UX maturity and can progress with effort and sponsorship
> C. Usability is not important for their products
> D. They should hire only external consultants

**Correct answer:** B — Low maturity is a starting point, not a permanent state. Progress requires effort and executive support.

---

## 🪤 Trap 5: Thinking Developers Do Not Need Usability Knowledge

**Trap:** Developers implementing interfaces need usability knowledge to build accessible, usable products.

**Example question:**
> A development team claims usability is "the designers' job" and they only need to implement the code. The problem is:
>
> A. They are correct — developers do not need usability knowledge
> B. Developers need usability knowledge to implement accessible markup, responsive design, and proper feedback
> C. Designers should write all the code
> D. Usability testing is unnecessary

**Correct answer:** B — Developers need usability knowledge for ARIA, semantic HTML, responsive design, and feedback implementation.
