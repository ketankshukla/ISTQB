# 🪤 Chapter 1 — Agile Principles and Mindset: Exam Traps

## 🪤 Trap 1: Thinking "Over" Means "Instead Of"

**Trap:** The Agile Manifesto values items on the left MORE than items on the right. It does not say the right side has no value.

**Example question:**
> The Agile Manifesto states that working software is valued over comprehensive documentation. This means:
>
> A. No documentation should be created
> B. Documentation has value but working software is more important
> C. Documentation is more important than working software
> D. Only user manuals should be created

**Correct answer:** B — "Over" means valued more, not "instead of." Documentation still has value.

---

## 🪤 Trap 2: Confusing Scrum Master with Project Manager

**Trap:** The Scrum Master is a servant leader, not a traditional project manager. They do not assign tasks or manage the team.

**Example question:**
> In Scrum, the Scrum Master's primary responsibility is to:
>
> A. Assign tasks to team members
> B. Remove impediments and facilitate Scrum practices
> C. Decide what the team will work on
> D. Evaluate individual team member performance

**Correct answer:** B — The Scrum Master facilitates, removes impediments, and coaches. They do not assign tasks (A), decide work (C), or evaluate individuals (D).

---

## 🪤 Trap 3: Thinking Changes Are Not Allowed During a Sprint

**Trap:** The Sprint Backlog should not change in scope, but the team can adapt HOW they achieve the Sprint Goal. The Product Owner can cancel a sprint in extreme cases.

**Example question:**
> During a sprint, the Product Owner discovers a critical customer issue. The team should:
>
> A. Ignore it until the next sprint
> B. Discuss with the PO and potentially adapt the sprint if the Sprint Goal is at risk
> C. Add it to the sprint without discussion
> D. Cancel the sprint immediately

**Correct answer:** B — The team and PO collaborate. If the new work threatens the Sprint Goal, the PO may cancel the sprint. Otherwise, the team adapts.

---

## 🪤 Trap 4: Thinking Kanban Has No Structure

**Trap:** Kanban has explicit policies, WIP limits, and visualization. It is not "do whatever you want."

**Example question:**
> A team adopting Kanban should:
>
> A. Work on as many items as possible simultaneously
> B. Visualize workflow, limit WIP, and manage flow
> C. Avoid any process rules
> D. Only use Kanban boards for tracking

**Correct answer:** B — Kanban has core practices: visualize, limit WIP, manage flow, make policies explicit.

---

## 🪤 Trap 5: Thinking Testers Are Not Needed in Agile

**Trap:** Testers are essential in agile. Their role evolves from "gatekeeper" to "quality coach, collaborator, and automation engineer."

**Example question:**
> In an agile team, the role of the tester:
>
> A. Is eliminated because developers test everything
> B. Evolves to include collaboration, automation, and quality coaching
> C. Becomes a separate team that tests after each sprint
> D. Is limited to writing test plans before development

**Correct answer:** B — Testers are essential in agile. Their role shifts but does not disappear.
