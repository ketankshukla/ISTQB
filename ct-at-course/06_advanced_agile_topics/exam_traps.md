# 🪤 Chapter 6 — Advanced Agile Topics: Exam Traps

## 🪤 Trap 1: Thinking SAFe Is the Only Way to Scale Agile

**Trap:** SAFe is one of several scaling frameworks (LeSS, Nexus, custom). The best choice depends on organization size, culture, and needs.

**Example question:**
> A company with 3 teams wants to scale agile. They must use SAFe. This statement is:
>
> A. True — SAFe is required for all scaled agile
> B. False — LeSS or Nexus may be more appropriate for smaller numbers of teams
> C. True only if the company is in the Fortune 500
> D. Required by the Scrum Guide

**Correct answer:** B — SAFe is designed for 50+ people. Smaller organizations may use LeSS or Nexus.

---

## 🪤 Trap 2: Thinking DevOps Is Just Tools

**Trap:** DevOps is primarily about culture and collaboration. Tools enable DevOps but are not DevOps itself.

**Example question:**
> A team buys Jenkins, Docker, and Kubernetes and calls themselves DevOps. They are missing:
>
> A. More expensive tools
> B. The cultural change of shared ownership and collaboration between dev and ops
> C. Better hardware
> D. A dedicated DevOps engineer

**Correct answer:** B — DevOps is culture first, tools second. Without collaboration, tools alone do not create DevOps.

---

## 🪤 Trap 3: Using Velocity to Compare Teams

**Trap:** Velocity is team-specific and should never be used to compare teams. Different teams have different baselines, story sizes, and contexts.

**Example question:**
> Team A has a velocity of 40 story points. Team B has a velocity of 25. Management concludes Team A is more productive. This conclusion is:
>
> A. Correct — higher velocity means higher productivity
> B. Incorrect — story points are not comparable across teams
> C. Correct for Scrum teams only
> D. Only true if both teams use the same tool

**Correct answer:** B — Story points and velocity are team-specific. A team's velocity is only meaningful for that team's capacity planning.

---

## 🪤 Trap 4: Thinking Continuous Testing Means Only Automated Testing

**Trap:** Continuous testing includes automated tests in the pipeline AND manual testing activities (exploratory, usability) that happen continuously.

**Example question:**
> Continuous testing means all testing is fully automated with no manual testing. This statement is:
>
> A. True — continuous testing requires 100% automation
> B. False — continuous testing integrates automated tests in the pipeline while still including manual testing like exploratory testing
> C. True only for DevOps teams
> D. The official ISTQB definition

**Correct answer:** B — Continuous testing integrates automated tests in the pipeline. Manual testing (exploratory, usability) still happens continuously.

---

## 🪤 Trap 5: Ignoring Metrics Context

**Trap:** Metrics without context can be misleading. Decreasing defects might mean better prevention or worse detection. Always look at trends and context.

**Example question:**
> A team's defect count increases from 5 to 15 per sprint. Management assumes quality is worsening. The tester notes the team also started doing more exploratory testing. The most likely explanation is:
>
> A. Quality is definitely worse
> B. More defects are being found because testing is more thorough, not because quality is worse
> C. Exploratory testing causes more defects
> D. The team should stop exploratory testing

**Correct answer:** B — More defects found may indicate better detection, not worse quality. Context matters.
