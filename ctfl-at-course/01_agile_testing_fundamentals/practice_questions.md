### 🔷 Q20 (AT-1.5, K2)

Which of the following is NOT a typical responsibility of an agile tester?

A. Designing acceptance tests with the product owner
B. Writing all production code for the team
C. Performing exploratory testing
D. Coaching the team on quality practices

---

## ✔️ Answers and Rationales

### 🔷 Q1
**Answer: C**

**Rationale:** The Agile Manifesto values: individuals and interactions over processes and tools, working software over comprehensive documentation, customer collaboration over contract negotiation, responding to change over following a plan.

### 🔷 Q2
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Early and continuous delivery requires early and continuous testing. This principle directly supports testing throughout development, not just at the end.

### 🔷 Q3
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** The whole-team approach means everyone contributes to quality, but testers still provide specialized testing skills. Testers are still needed (A is wrong), don't need to become developers (C is wrong), and don't work in a separate team (D is wrong).

### 🔷 Q4
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Handing all code to the tester at the end creates a "mini-waterfall" within the sprint. It delays feedback, prevents early defect detection, and contradicts agile principles of early and continuous testing.

### 🔷 Q5
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Three Amigos meetings involve business, developer, and tester discussing a story. The tester's role is to clarify acceptance criteria and identify edge cases, ensuring testability before development begins.

### 🔷 Q6
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Independent testing provides objectivity, which is valuable for high-risk or regulated features. However, it does not eliminate team-based testing (A is wrong), and it typically reduces feedback speed (C is wrong).

### 🔷 Q7
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Working software as the primary measure means demonstrating that features work (via tests) is more important than completing documentation. This does not eliminate documentation (A is wrong) or testing (D is wrong).

### 🔷 Q8
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** In agile, all test levels occur continuously throughout the sprint. They are not sequential phases (A) or end-of-release activities (C). TDD does not replace other testing (D).

### 🔷 Q9
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Pairing with a developer is a collaborative practice that enables immediate feedback and shared understanding. It does not eliminate independent testing (A), nor does it indicate the tester has nothing to do (C).

### 🔷 Q10
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Principle 2 explicitly states: "Welcome changing requirements, even late in development."

### 🔷 Q11
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** In agile, system testing is continuous, not a phase. Each completed story is system-tested as part of the sprint, ensuring the increment is always potentially releasable.

### 🔷 Q12
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Agile testers must collaborate closely with developers and business representatives. Isolation (A) and detailed plans (C) are traditional practices, not agile.

### 🔷 Q13
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Agile values working software over comprehensive documentation, but regulatory needs may require documentation. The team should create just-enough documentation to satisfy requirements without unnecessary overhead.

### 🔷 Q14
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Individuals and interactions over processes and tools implies direct collaboration is preferred over formal processes like defect reports for all communication.

### 🔷 Q15
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** The Definition of Done in agile typically includes coding, testing, reviewing, and integration — ensuring the feature is potentially releasable, not just coded.

### 🔷 Q16
**Answer: A**

**Rationale:** Helping developers with unit tests and reviewing acceptance criteria demonstrates coaching and collaboration — key aspects of the agile tester as quality advocate. Testers are not gatekeepers (B) or project managers (D).

### 🔷 Q17
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Principle 8 states: "Agile processes promote sustainable development." Testing must be sustainable, not a crunch-time activity at sprint end.

### 🔷 Q18
**Answer: A**

**Rationale:** A story is only "done" when tested and working. This aligns with working software as the primary measure of progress. Untested code is not working software.

### 🔷 Q19
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Collaboration between testers and developers enables early defect detection, shared understanding of requirements, and faster feedback cycles.

### 🔷 Q20
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Agile testers do not write production code. They design tests, perform exploratory testing, and coach on quality. Production code is written by developers.
