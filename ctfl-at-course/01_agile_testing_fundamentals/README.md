## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Describe the Agile Manifesto and its values and principles
2. Explain how agile software development differs from traditional sequential development
3. Describe the importance of early and continuous testing in agile
4. Explain the concept of whole-team approach to quality
5. Describe the role and skills of a tester in an agile team
6. Compare independent testing with team-based testing in agile

---

## Key Concepts to Master

- The 4 values and 12 principles of the Agile Manifesto
- How agile values affect testing practices
- The whole-team approach to quality
- Tester's role in agile teams (not just executing tests)
- Benefits and drawbacks of independent testing vs. team-based testing
- How testing differs in agile: continuous, collaborative, just-in-time

---

## Exam Tips

- Expect questions testing your understanding of agile values vs. traditional practices
- Know how each agile principle relates to testing
- The whole-team approach is frequently tested — know what it means and what it doesn't mean
- Be able to compare agile testing with traditional testing for specific activities
