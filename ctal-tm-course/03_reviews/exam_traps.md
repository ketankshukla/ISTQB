# Chapter 3 — Reviews: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Confusing Walkthrough with Inspection

**Trap:** Walkthroughs are led by the author and relatively informal. Inspections are led by a trained moderator, use checklists, and are highly formal.

**Example question:**
> A document is examined by a team using a checklist, led by a trained moderator who is not the author, with metrics collected. This is:
>
> A. An informal review
> B. A walkthrough
> C. A technical review
> D. An inspection

**Correct answer:** D — Checklists, trained independent moderator, and metrics collection are hallmarks of an inspection.

---

## Trap 2: Solving Problems in the Review Meeting

**Trap:** Reviews identify defects; solutions are developed after the meeting. Trying to solve problems during the review wastes time and reduces defect detection.

**Example question:**
> During a requirements review meeting, a reviewer identifies an ambiguity. The team spends 20 minutes discussing how to resolve it. This is:
>
> A. Good practice — the defect is being fixed immediately
> B. Poor practice — solutions should be developed outside the review meeting
> C. Required for all formal reviews
> D. Only appropriate for walkthroughs

**Correct answer:** B — Review meetings should identify defects, not solve them. Solution discussions derail the meeting and reduce efficiency.

---

## Trap 3: Management Attendance at Formal Reviews

**Trap:** Management does not attend technical reviews or inspections to avoid creating pressure on reviewers.

**Example question:**
> The project manager wants to attend a technical review to monitor progress. The test manager should:
>
> A. Welcome the project manager — their presence improves review quality
> B. Explain that management attendance is typically excluded from formal reviews to maintain objectivity
> C. Allow attendance but only if the manager promises not to speak
> D. Cancel the review if the manager cannot attend

**Correct answer:** B — Management presence can pressure reviewers and reduce honest defect reporting.

---

## Trap 4: Thinking All Documents Need the Same Review Formality

**Trap:** Match review formality to document risk and importance. Low-risk documents don't need inspections.

**Example question:**
> A team is writing internal documentation for a low-risk internal tool. The MOST appropriate review type is:
>
> A. Inspection
> B. Technical review
> C. Walkthrough or informal review
> D. No review needed

**Correct answer:** C — Low-risk internal documentation benefits from a walkthrough or informal review. Inspection would be excessive.

---

## Trap 5: Forgetting That Reviews Find Defects Early

**Trap:** Reviews (static testing) find defects before dynamic testing. Early defect detection is cheaper.

**Example question:**
> A defect found during a requirements review typically costs less to fix than the same defect found:
>
> A. During unit testing
> B. During system testing
> C. In production
> D. All of the above

**Correct answer:** D — Defects found earlier are always cheaper to fix. Requirements review is earlier than all dynamic test levels.
