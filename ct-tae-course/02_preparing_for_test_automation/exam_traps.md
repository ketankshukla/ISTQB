# 🪤 Chapter 2 — Preparing for Test Automation: Exam Traps

## 🪤 Trap 1: Confusing PoC with PoV

**Trap:** PoC verifies technical feasibility. PoV demonstrates business value. They are not the same.

**Example question:**
> A team installs a tool, automates 3 test cases, and verifies it works with their application. This is:
>
> A. A Proof of Value
> B. A Proof of Concept
> C. A pilot project
> D. A full deployment

**Correct answer:** B — This describes a PoC (technical feasibility). A PoV would measure business value (time saved, defects found).

---

## 🪤 Trap 2: Ignoring TCO and Focusing Only on License Cost

**Trap:** The initial license price is only part of the cost. Training, infrastructure, maintenance, and support must be included.

**Example question:**
> A tool has a free open-source license. Therefore, its Total Cost of Ownership is zero. This statement is:
>
> A. True — open source is always free
> B. False — TCO includes training, infrastructure, and maintenance
> C. True — there are no costs for open-source tools
> D. False — only because the team needs to buy support

**Correct answer:** B — TCO includes much more than the license. Even free tools require training, infrastructure, and maintenance time.

---

## 🪤 Trap 3: Selecting a Tool Based on Popularity Alone

**Trap:** The most popular tool is not necessarily the best fit for a specific context. Technical fit, team skills, and integration needs matter more.

**Example question:**
> A team selects a test tool because "everyone in the industry uses it." The MAIN risk is:
>
> A. The tool is too expensive
> B. The tool may not fit the team's specific technical requirements
> C. The tool has too many features
> D. The vendor will go out of business

**Correct answer:** B — Popularity does not guarantee fit. The tool must support the SUT technology and integrate with the existing toolchain.

---

## 🪤 Trap 4: Thinking a PoC Proves Business Value

**Trap:** A PoC proves the tool CAN work technically. It does NOT prove it SHOULD be adopted from a business perspective.

**Example question:**
> After a successful PoC, management asks "Will this save us money?" The TAE should:
>
> A. Say yes because the PoC was successful
> B. Explain that a PoV is needed to demonstrate business value and ROI
> C. Deploy immediately without further analysis
> D. Reject the tool because the PoC does not answer that question

**Correct answer:** B — A PoV is specifically designed to measure business value and ROI.

---

## 🪤 Trap 5: Automating Everything Without Scope Definition

**Trap:** Without clear scope definition, automation efforts grow uncontrollably, leading to over-budget and delayed projects.

**Example question:**
> A team begins automating without defining which tests to automate. The MOST likely consequence is:
>
> A. They will finish early
> B. Scope creep will lead to missed deadlines and budget overruns
> C. They will automate exactly the right tests
> D. Management will be pleased with the thoroughness

**Correct answer:** B — Undefined scope leads to scope creep. Clear requirements and prioritization are essential.
