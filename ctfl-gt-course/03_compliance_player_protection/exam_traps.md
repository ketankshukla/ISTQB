# Chapter 3 — Compliance and Player Protection: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Confusing Self-Exclusion with Time-Out

**Trap:** Self-exclusion is long-term (months to years). Time-out is short-term (hours to weeks). They are different measures.

**Example question:**
> A player wants to stop gambling for 6 months. They should use:
>
> A. A time-out
> B. Self-exclusion
> C. A deposit limit
> D. A loss limit

**Correct answer:** B — Self-exclusion is the long-term voluntary ban. Time-outs are temporary (typically 24 hours to 6 weeks).

---

## Trap 2: Confusing Player Protection with Fraud Prevention

**Trap:** Player protection (self-exclusion, limits, reality checks) protects the PLAYER from harm. Fraud prevention (collusion, bonus abuse, money laundering) protects the OPERATOR. They are different objectives.

**Example question:**
> Requiring a player to set a maximum deposit amount is an example of:
>
> A. Fraud prevention
> B. Player protection
> C. AML compliance
> D. Game fairness testing

**Correct answer:** B — Deposit limits are a player protection measure. They help prevent problem gambling, not fraud.

---

## Trap 3: Thinking KYC Is Only About Age Verification

**Trap:** KYC includes identity verification, address verification, age verification, AND source of funds checks (for high-value players).

**Example question:**
> Which of the following is NOT typically part of KYC in gambling?
>
> A. Verifying the player's identity with government ID
> B. Verifying the player's address with a utility bill
> C. Testing the RNG for statistical randomness
> D. Verifying the player's age

**Correct answer:** C — RNG testing is a game fairness activity, not part of KYC. KYC is about knowing the customer, not testing the game.

---

## Trap 4: Confusing Regulators with Testing Labs

**Trap:** Regulators (UKGC, MGA) issue licenses and enforce rules. Testing labs (GLI, eCOGRA, iTech) test and certify products. They are different organizations.

**Example question:**
> Which organization is responsible for issuing gambling licenses in Great Britain?
>
> A. GLI
> B. eCOGRA
> C. UK Gambling Commission
> D. iTech Labs

**Correct answer:** C — UKGC is the regulator. GLI, eCOGRA, and iTech Labs are independent testing/certification organizations.

---

## Trap 5: Thinking All Jurisdictions Have the Same Requirements

**Trap:** Gambling regulations vary significantly by jurisdiction. What is required in the UK may not be required in Curaçao.

**Example question:**
> An operator wants to launch in both the UK and Sweden. Which statement is TRUE?
>
> A. They can use the same responsible gaming procedures in both jurisdictions
> B. They must comply with the specific requirements of each jurisdiction, which may differ
> C. They only need to comply with the stricter of the two sets of requirements
> D. Sweden automatically accepts UKGC licensing

**Correct answer:** B — Each jurisdiction has its own requirements. For example, Sweden restricts bonuses and requires Spelpaus integration, while the UK requires GAMSTOP and bans credit cards.
