## Answers and Rationales

### Q1
**Answer: C**

**Rationale:** In poker, players compete against each other. The house earns through rake. In slots, roulette, and blackjack, players bet against the house.

### Q2
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** House Edge = 100% - RTP = 100% - 95% = 5%.

### Q3
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Hit frequency = proportion of rounds with any win. RTP = proportion of wagered money returned over time. They are independent metrics.

### Q4
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Exchange betting allows players to bet against each other; the operator takes commission. In lottery, slots, and bingo, players bet against the operator.

### Q5
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Same RTP means same long-term return. Volatility describes win size and frequency distribution. High volatility = larger, rarer wins.

### Q6
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** UK Gambling Commission (UKGC) regulates gambling in Great Britain. MGA = Malta. NGCB = Nevada. eCOGRA is a testing lab, not a regulator.

### Q7
**Answer: C**

**Rationale:** GLI (Gaming Laboratories International) is an independent testing lab that certifies RNGs and game mathematics. UKGC and MGA are regulators. Spelinspektionen is the Swedish regulator.

### Q8
**Answer: C**

**Rationale:** Poker involves significant skill in strategy, reading opponents, and probability. Roulette, slots, and scratch cards are primarily games of chance.

### Q9
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** RTP = return over wagered amount. 98% of $1,000 = $980 expected return over a very large number of plays.

### Q10
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Game studios develop games and license them to operators. Operators hold licenses and accept wagers. Regulators oversee activities. Testing labs certify products.

### Q11
**Answer: C**

**Rationale:** Pool betting / parimutuel pools all stakes; winners share the pool minus commission. Fixed-odds = set odds. Exchange = player vs player. Spread = accuracy-based payoff.

### Q12
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Hit frequency is the percentage of game rounds resulting in any win. It does not indicate RTP, house edge, or the size of wins.

### Q13
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** eCOGRA (eCommerce and Online Gaming Regulation and Assurance) is an independent testing and certification body focused on online gambling fairness and player protection.

### Q14
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Verifying fairness and randomness is a core gambling-specific testing objective. Button clickability, page load speed, and brand colors are general testing concerns.

### Q15
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** RTP = (Total Payout / Total Wagered) x 100 = ($960,000 / $1,000,000) x 100 = 96%.

### Q16
**Answer: C**

**Rationale:** Lottery involves purchasing tickets and waiting for scheduled number draws. Casino, sports betting, and poker do not follow this model.

### Q17
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** A white-label platform is provided by one company (technology supplier) but branded and operated by another (the operator).

### Q18
**Answer: C**

**Rationale:** American roulette has a 5.26% house edge (due to 0 and 00). European roulette = 2.7%. Blackjack basic strategy = ~0.5%. Baccarat banker bet = ~1.06%.

### Q19
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Third-party certification ensures game fairness and regulatory compliance. It does not reduce costs, speed up releases, or eliminate internal testing.

### Q20
**Answer: B**

**Rationale:** Live dealer games use physical elements (real cards, dice, roulette wheels) streamed in real time, combined with software for betting interfaces.
