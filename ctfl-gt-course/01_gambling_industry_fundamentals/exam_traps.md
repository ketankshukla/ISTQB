# Chapter 1 — Gambling Industry Fundamentals: Exam Traps

## Trap 1: Confusing RTP with Hit Frequency

**Trap:** RTP and hit frequency are completely different metrics.

**Example question:**
> A slot game has an RTP of 96% and a hit frequency of 12%. Which statement is TRUE?
>
> A. The player wins 96% of the time
> B. The player wins money on 12% of spins, and the average return is 96% of wagered money over time
> C. The player loses 88% of the time
> D. The game pays out $0.96 on every $1 spin

**Correct answer:** B — Hit frequency = how often you win. RTP = how much you win relative to wager over time.

**Wrong answers:**
- A: RTP is not win frequency
- C: Losing 88% of spins confuses hit frequency with loss rate
- D: RTP is a long-term average, not per-spin guarantee

## Trap 2: Confusing House Edge and RTP

**Trap:** House edge = 100% - RTP. They are inversely related but not the same concept.

**Example question:**
> A game has an RTP of 97.3%. What is the house edge?
>
> A. 97.3%
> B. 2.7%
> C. 100%
> D. Cannot be calculated

**Correct answer:** B — House Edge = 100% - 97.3% = 2.7%.

---

## Trap 3: Thinking All Casino Games Have the Same RTP

**Trap:** Different games, variants, and even individual machines can have different RTPs.

**Example question:**
> Which of the following typically has the LOWEST house edge?
>
> A. American roulette
> B. European roulette
> C. Blackjack with basic strategy
> D. A slot machine with 94% RTP

**Correct answer:** C — Blackjack with basic strategy has a house edge around 0.5%, lower than European roulette (~2.7%), American roulette (~5.26%), and a 94% RTP slot (6% house edge).

---

## Trap 4: Confusing Volatility with RTP

**Trap:** Two games can have the same RTP but very different volatility.

**Example question:**
> Game A and Game B both have 96% RTP. Game A has low volatility; Game B has high volatility. Which is TRUE?
>
> A. Game A pays more money over time
> B. Game B is more likely to bankrupt a player's bankroll quickly
> C. Game A has a higher RTP
> D. Game B pays out more frequently

**Correct answer:** B — High volatility means larger, rarer wins = higher risk of rapid bankroll depletion. Both have same RTP.

---

## Trap 5: Thinking the House Edge Guarantees Profit on Every Bet

**Trap:** House edge is a long-term statistical average, not a guarantee on individual bets or sessions.

**Example question:**
> A casino game has a 5% house edge. Which statement is TRUE?
>
> A. The casino wins exactly $0.05 on every $1 bet
> B. The casino is guaranteed to profit in every 24-hour period
> C. Over millions of bets, the casino expects to retain approximately 5% of all wagers
> D. No player can ever win money on this game

**Correct answer:** C — House edge is a long-term expectation, not a per-bet guarantee.
