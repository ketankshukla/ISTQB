## Rationales

### Q1
**B.** Generative AI creates new content. Classification is discriminative. Explicit rules describe traditional programming. LLMs process natural language.

### Q2
**C.** Underrepresentation of diverse names is bias. Hallucination would involve inventing naming rules. Reasoning error would involve logical mistakes.

### Q3
**B.** Chain-of-thought is ideal for nested conditional logic. Zero-shot may miss conditions. Removing constraints or increasing temperature worsens results.

### Q4
**B.** Exposing credentials is a security and privacy breach. Hallucination, environmental impact, and reasoning errors are unrelated.

### Q5
**B.** The vector database and retrieval logic find relevant chunks. The LLM generates responses. The embedding model creates vectors. Prompt engineering doesn't retrieve.

### Q6
**B.** Catastrophic forgetting is specific to fine-tuning. Outdated docs, irrelevant retrieval, and context limits are RAG concerns.

### Q7
**B.** Missing an alternative flow violates completeness. The test case may be correct, consistent, and feasible — but incomplete.

### Q8
**B.** ISO/IEC 42001 is the AI management system standard. ISO 25059 is quality model. EU AI Act is regulation. OWASP is security risks.

### Q9
**B.** System prompts set persistent framework context across sessions. User prompts are per-query. Model size and context window don't enforce conventions.

### Q10
**B.** A/B testing compares prompt variants. Red teaming is adversarial testing. Cost management tracks spend. Distillation creates smaller models.

### Q11
**B.** Inventing a non-existent certification is hallucination. Context window causes truncation. Bias involves systematic skew. Temperature affects randomness.

### Q12
**B.** Right-sizing and green hosting reduce carbon footprint. Multiple models, always-latest, and disabling caching increase impact.

### Q13
**C.** Scaling establishes standards and governance. Assessment analyzes readiness. Pilot runs experiments. Maturity optimizes.

### Q14
**B.** Tiered training and mentorship address skill gaps constructively. Mandates, exclusion, and reassignment are poor practices.

### Q15
**B.** RAG with citation provides traceability to source sections. Fine-tuning, zero-shot, and discriminative models don't provide provenance.

### Q16
**B.** All six components (role, task, input, constraints, examples, format) are present. Budget and API docs are irrelevant.

### Q17
**B.** Incorrect calculation is a reasoning error. Hallucination would involve inventing metrics. Bias involves systematic skew. Context window causes truncation.

### Q18
**B.** Attacker directly providing malicious instructions is direct prompt injection. Indirect uses manipulated documents. Model inversion extracts data. Data poisoning affects training.

### Q19
**B.** NIST AI RMF is US guidance with Govern/Map/Measure/Manage. It is not EU regulation, doesn't replace ISO 42001, and doesn't prohibit government AI.

### Q20
**B.** Prompt versioning and token tracking directly investigate cost increases. Hallucination rates, red teaming, and fine-tuning quality don't explain cost spikes.

### Q21
**B.** In-context learning adapts from prompt examples without weight updates. Training data updates, fine-tuning, and retrieval are different mechanisms.

### Q22
**B.** RAG with re-ingestion handles frequent updates without retraining. Weekly fine-tuning is impractical. Zero-shot lacks knowledge. Custom models are overkill.

### Q23
**B.** Accountability and regulatory requirements demand human validation. Cost, pilot-phase-only, and daily limits are incorrect reasons.

### Q24
**B.** Pilot involves controlled experiments with metrics. Simultaneous deployment, eliminating tools, and organization-wide fine-tuning are premature.

### Q25
**B.** Smaller models and caching address latency. Large models and ensembles add latency. Storing history in weights is unrelated and a privacy risk.

### Q26
**A, C.** Synthetic data can reproduce real patterns and carries re-identification risk. B and D are false statements.

### Q27
**B.** Communication about augmentation and reskilling is constructive. Elimination timelines, dismissal, and outsourcing are poor practices.

### Q28
**B.** System prompts set session behavior; user prompts are specific queries. Encryption, training-only use, and length limits are incorrect.

### Q29
**B.** Defect descriptions may contain PII. Perfect accuracy, inability to generate paragraphs, and irrelevant environmental impact are false.

### Q30
**B.** Lower temperature increases determinism. Training data size, no effect, and guaranteed accuracy are incorrect.

### Q31
**A.** Distribution monitoring detects drift and quality changes. Parameter count, color scheme, and office temperature are irrelevant.

### Q32
**B.** Stable format + large dataset = good fine-tuning case. RAG is for dynamic knowledge. Zero-shot wouldn't learn format. Spreadsheets lack flexibility.

### Q33
**B.** Iterative refinement means improving prompts based on evaluation. Human oversight is separate. Static and no-context prompting are anti-patterns.

### Q34
**B.** Prompt chaining handles documents exceeding context windows. Fine-tuning doesn't solve length. Temperature doesn't compress. Discriminative models don't analyze.

### Q35
**B.** RAG is preferred when knowledge changes frequently and provenance matters. RAG doesn't update weights. Infrastructure and cost claims are situational.

### Q36
**A.** Concrete examples directly address vagueness. Temperature, removing constraints, and shortening format worsen the problem.

### Q37
**C.** OWASP LLM Top 10 lists LLM security risks. ISO 25059 is quality. EU AI Act is regulation. NIST RMF is risk management guidance.

### Q38
**B.** Prompt libraries, quality gates, and accountability ensure consistency. Unregulated individual use, eliminating docs, and preventing sharing create inconsistency.

### Q39
**B.** Incorrect logic about boundary values is a reasoning error. Hallucination would involve inventing fields. Bias involves systematic skew. Privacy breach involves exposing data.

### Q40
**B.** Augmentation and focus on high-value work aligns with ISTQB principles. Elimination, redundancy, and headcount reduction messages are incorrect.
