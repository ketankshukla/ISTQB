# 🪤 Chapter 5 — Exam Traps

## 🪤 Trap 1: Technology-Only Adoption

**Trap:** Answers that treat GenAI adoption as purely a technical implementation (buy tools, train models) without addressing people, process, and governance.

**Defense:** The syllabus emphasizes roadmap, change management, training, governance, and cultural adaptation. Technical implementation alone is insufficient.

## 🪤 Trap 2: Skipping the Pilot Phase

**Trap:** Answers that recommend immediate organization-wide rollout without piloting, metrics, or validation.

**Defense:** A phased roadmap with pilot experimentation is essential. The syllabus expects understanding of staged adoption: assessment -> pilot -> scaling -> maturity.

## 🪤 Trap 3: Ignoring Role Anxiety

**Trap:** Answers that dismiss tester concerns about job security or assume everyone will embrace GenAI enthusiastically.

**Defense:** Change management must explicitly address fear, provide clear communication that GenAI augments rather than replaces, and offer reskilling pathways.

## 🪤 Trap 4: Removing Human Oversight During Scaling

**Trap:** Answers suggesting that as the organization gains confidence, human review can be eliminated for "routine" GenAI outputs.

**Defense:** Human oversight is a syllabus principle that applies at all maturity levels. Scaling means more efficient oversight, not no oversight.

## 🪤 Trap 5: Conflating Adoption Roadmap with Project Plan

**Trap:** Answers that describe a simple Gantt chart of tool deployment without the assessment, experimentation, governance, and cultural components.

**Defense:** A GenAI adoption roadmap includes capability assessment, use case prioritization, risk review, pilot metrics, training, governance, and continuous optimization — not just tool installation.

## 🪤 Trap 6: Treating All Use Cases Equally

**Trap:** Answers that suggest deploying GenAI across all testing activities simultaneously.

**Defense:** Prioritization matters. Start with high-value, low-risk, well-understood use cases. Safety-critical and compliance-sensitive areas need more caution.

## 🪤 Trap 7: Ignoring Governance at Scale

**Trap:** Answers that describe scaling GenAI use without mentioning governance frameworks, policies, or accountability.

**Defense:** Governance is a core component of the scaling phase. Without it, organizations face uncontrolled costs, data exposure, inconsistent quality, and compliance risk.
